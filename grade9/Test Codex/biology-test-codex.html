Grade 9 Biology: Photosynthesis Learning & Practice

Introduction: Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants, algae, and some bacteria make their own food using light energy. It is a foundational concept in Biology because it connects energy flow, plant structure, and life on Earth.

Key Concepts

  • Photosynthesis: Conversion of light energy into chemical energy stored in glucose.
  • Chloroplast: Organelle in plant cells where photosynthesis occurs.
  • Chlorophyll: Green pigment that absorbs light, especially red and blue wavelengths.
  • Reactants: Carbon dioxide (CO₂) and water (H₂O).
  • Products: Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆) and oxygen (O₂).

Main Structures and Processes

  • Leaves are broad and thin to capture light efficiently.
  • Stomata are tiny pores that allow CO₂ to enter and O₂ to exit.
  • Xylem transports water from roots to leaves.
  • In chloroplasts, light-dependent reactions capture light energy, and other reactions use that energy to build glucose.

Why It Matters

Photosynthesis provides food for plants, which supports food chains in ecosystems. It also releases oxygen needed for aerobic respiration in many organisms, including humans, and helps reduce atmospheric CO₂.

Summary: Photosynthesis is how plants transform light, water, and carbon dioxide into glucose and oxygen. This process powers ecosystems and supports most life by supplying both energy-rich food and oxygen.
