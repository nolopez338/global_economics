Context: A machine fills bottles with cleaning solution. Let $X$ be the random fill amount (in mL) above a baseline measurement.

Given data: $X \sim \text{Uniform}(0,10)$ and the density is constant on the support.

Task: Find the density rule, evaluate $P(2 \le X \le 7)$, and explain what that probability means as area under the graph.

↑

Summary model statement: The variable is continuous, the support is $[0,10]$, and all equal-length intervals have equal probability because the density is flat.

Template structure note: In every problem card, keep this order: description, C1 summary, C2 calculations, and C2 interpretation so future editors can map prompts to criteria consistently.

↑

For $X \sim \text{Uniform}(0,10)$,

$$ f(x)= \begin{cases} \frac{1}{10}, & 0 \le x \le 10 \\ 0, & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} $$

Then

$P(2 \le X \le 7)=\frac{7-2}{10}=\frac{5}{10}=0.5$.

Using an integral gives the same result:

$$ P(2 \le X \le 7)=\int_{2}^{7} \frac{1}{10}\,dx=\frac{1}{10}(7-2)=0.5. $$
↑

The support interval $[0,10]$ means values outside this interval have probability $0$.

The shaded area from $x=2$ to $x=7$ represents the event $2 \le X \le 7$. A wider shaded interval produces a greater area and therefore a greater probability.

Context: A delivery app tracks waiting time $X$ (minutes) for same-zone orders.

Given data:

$$ f(x)= \begin{cases} 0.12, & 0 \le x < 5 \\ 0.04, & 5 \le x \le 10 \\ 0, & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} $$

Task: Verify this is a valid density and compute $P(2\le X\le 8)$.

↑

The support is $[0,10]$ and the density has two flat pieces with different heights.

This layout models two service modes: faster deliveries are more common ($0\le x<5$ has higher density), while longer waits are less common ($5\le x\le10$ has lower density).

↑

Check total area:

$$ \int_{0}^{5}0.12\,dx+\int_{5}^{10}0.04\,dx=(5)(0.12)+(5)(0.04)=0.6+0.2=0.8. $$

Because the area is $0.8\ne1$, this specific rule is not a valid density. A corrected version would scale heights (for example, $0.15$ and $0.05$).

Using the corrected model:

$$ P(2\le X\le 8)=\int_{2}^{5}0.15\,dx+\int_{5}^{8}0.05\,dx=3(0.15)+3(0.05)=0.60. $$
↑

In a graph, the first rectangle is taller than the second. The taller height means more probability per minute in that interval.

The shaded area from $2$ to $8$ crosses both pieces, so it must be split into two regions and added.

Context: A graph shows a flat density $f(x)=\frac{1}{8}$ on $[1,9]$. The shaded band is between $x=3$ and $x=6$.

Task: Write the event in symbols and state its probability.

↑

The shaded region corresponds to the interval event $3\le X\le 6$.

The full support width is $9-1=8$, so each unit of width contributes equal probability weight because the density is constant.

↑

$P(3\le X\le 6)=\frac{6-3}{9-1}=\frac{3}{8}=0.375$.

Equivalent integral form:

$$ P(3\le X\le 6)=\int_{3}^{6}\frac{1}{8}\,dx=\frac{3}{8}. $$
↑

The support interval in the graph tells where bars or curve segments can appear; outside $[1,9]$ there is no area and no probability.

The shaded area under a density graph is probability, not height alone. A short interval with high height can compete with a wide interval with low height.

Context: Model A is uniform on $[0,10]$ with $f_A(x)=\frac{1}{10}$. Model B has a sloped density $f_B(x)=\frac{x}{50}$ for $0\le x\le10$.

Task: Compare $P(X\ge 8)$ under both models and interpret the graph-level difference.

↑

Both models share the same support $[0,10]$, but they distribute probability differently.

Model A is flat (equal weight everywhere), while Model B increases with $x$, assigning more weight to larger values.

↑

Uniform model:

$$ P_A(X\ge8)=\int_{8}^{10}\frac{1}{10}\,dx=\frac{2}{10}=0.2. $$

Sloped model:

$$ P_B(X\ge8)=\int_{8}^{10}\frac{x}{50}\,dx=\left.\frac{x^2}{100}\right|_{8}^{10}=\frac{100-64}{100}=0.36. $$
↑

The flat graph keeps constant height, so probability depends only on interval width.

The sloped graph rises toward the right, so the region near $x=10$ has greater average height. That is why $P_B(X\ge8)$ is larger than $P_A(X\ge8)$ even with the same width.

Context: Let $X\sim\text{Uniform}(a,b)$ with $a=1$ and unknown $b>1$.

Given data: $P(X\le5)=0.5$.

Task: Find $b$, write the full density function, and create one interpretation sentence that could accompany a graph.

↑

The model is uniform, so probabilities depend on relative interval lengths within support $[1,b]$.

Because $P(X\le5)=0.5$, the cutoff $x=5$ splits the support area into two equal halves.

↑

For a uniform variable,

$$ P(X\le5)=\frac{5-1}{b-1}=\frac{4}{b-1}=0.5. $$

So $b-1=8$ and $b=9$.

The resulting density is

$$ f(x)= \begin{cases} \frac{1}{8}, & 1 \le x \le 9 \\ 0, & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} $$
↑

On the graph of this density, the rectangle from $1$ to $5$ has the same area as the rectangle from $5$ to $9$, so each side represents probability $0.5$.
