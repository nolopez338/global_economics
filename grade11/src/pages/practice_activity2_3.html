A regional policy unit surveys 180 exporters about a new trade agreement. In the sample, 117 exporters say they support the agreement.

1) Construct and interpret a 95% confidence interval for the population proportion of exporters who support the agreement.

2) What minimum sample size is needed to estimate the support rate with a margin of error no larger than \(E = 0.04\) at 95% confidence?

↑

(C7) Constructs the confidence interval for a proportion.

Step 1: Identify \(x=117\), \(n=180\), and \(\hat{p} = 117/180 = 0.65\).

Step 2: Large-sample conditions.

\[ n\hat{p} = 180(0.65) = 117 \ge 10, \qquad n(1-\hat{p}) = 180(0.35) = 63 \ge 10 \]

Step 3: Standard error.

\[ SE = \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.65(0.35)}{180}} \approx 0.0356 \]

Step 4: Margin of error (95% confidence, \(z_{\alpha/2}=1.96\)).

\[ E = 1.96(0.0356) \approx 0.0697 \]

Step 5: Confidence interval.

\[ \hat{p} \pm E = 0.65 \pm 0.0697 \Rightarrow [0.580,\ 0.720] \]

Interpretation: We are 95% confident that about 58.0% to 72.0% of all exporters in the region support the agreement.

(C6) Stablishes the sample size so the error will not exceed a specified amount.

Step 1: Target error and confidence. The goal is \(E = 0.04\) at 95% confidence, so \(z_{\alpha/2}=1.96\).

Step 2: Choose \(p^*\). Use \(p^* = \hat{p} = 0.65\) because the sample provides a reasonable prior estimate.

Step 3: Apply the proportion sample-size formula.

\[ n \ge \frac{z_{\alpha/2}^2\,p^*(1-p^*)}{E^2} = \frac{1.96^2(0.65)(0.35)}{0.04^2} = \frac{0.8740}{0.0016} \approx 546.3 \]

Step 4: Round up and interpret. Round up to \(n = 547\). Sampling at least 547 exporters keeps the margin of error at or below 0.04.

A financial inclusion survey asks 240 households whether they used mobile money for remittances in the past year. 90 households report using it.

1) Construct and interpret a 90% confidence interval for the population proportion of households using mobile money for remittances.

2) What minimum sample size is needed so the margin of error is at most \(E = 0.03\) at 90% confidence?

↑

(C7) Constructs the confidence interval for a proportion.

Step 1: Identify \(x=90\), \(n=240\), and \(\hat{p} = 90/240 = 0.375\).

Step 2: Large-sample conditions.

\[ n\hat{p} = 240(0.375) = 90 \ge 10, \qquad n(1-\hat{p}) = 240(0.625) = 150 \ge 10 \]

Step 3: Standard error.

\[ SE = \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.375(0.625)}{240}} \approx 0.0313 \]

Step 4: Margin of error (90% confidence, \(z_{\alpha/2}=1.645\)).

\[ E = 1.645(0.0313) \approx 0.0514 \]

Step 5: Confidence interval.

\[ \hat{p} \pm E = 0.375 \pm 0.0514 \Rightarrow [0.324,\ 0.426] \]

Interpretation: We are 90% confident that about 32.4% to 42.6% of households use mobile money for remittances.

(C6) Stablishes the sample size so the error will not exceed a specified amount.

Step 1: Target error and confidence. The goal is \(E = 0.03\) at 90% confidence, so \(z_{\alpha/2}=1.645\).

Step 2: Choose \(p^*\). Use \(p^* = \hat{p} = 0.375\) because the pilot sample provides an estimate.

Step 3: Apply the proportion sample-size formula.

\[ n \ge \frac{z_{\alpha/2}^2\,p^*(1-p^*)}{E^2} = \frac{1.645^2(0.375)(0.625)}{0.03^2} = \frac{0.6342}{0.0009} \approx 704.7 \]

Step 4: Round up and interpret. Round up to \(n = 705\). Sampling at least 705 households keeps the margin of error within 0.03.

A market watch group surveys 150 firms about whether they expect the local currency to depreciate within six months. 102 firms say they expect depreciation.

1) Construct and interpret a 98% confidence interval for the population proportion of firms expecting depreciation.

2) What minimum sample size is needed for a margin of error no larger than \(E = 0.02\) at 98% confidence?

↑

(C7) Constructs the confidence interval for a proportion.

Step 1: Identify \(x=102\), \(n=150\), and \(\hat{p} = 102/150 = 0.68\).

Step 2: Large-sample conditions.

\[ n\hat{p} = 150(0.68) = 102 \ge 10, \qquad n(1-\hat{p}) = 150(0.32) = 48 \ge 10 \]

Step 3: Standard error.

\[ SE = \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.68(0.32)}{150}} \approx 0.0381 \]

Step 4: Margin of error (98% confidence, \(z_{\alpha/2}=2.326\)).

\[ E = 2.326(0.0381) \approx 0.0885 \]

Step 5: Confidence interval.

\[ \hat{p} \pm E = 0.68 \pm 0.0885 \Rightarrow [0.592,\ 0.769] \]

Interpretation: We are 98% confident that about 59.2% to 76.9% of firms expect depreciation.

(C6) Stablishes the sample size so the error will not exceed a specified amount.

Step 1: Target error and confidence. The goal is \(E = 0.02\) at 98% confidence, so \(z_{\alpha/2}=2.326\).

Step 2: Choose \(p^*\). Use \(p^* = \hat{p} = 0.68\) because the sample provides the best available estimate.

Step 3: Apply the proportion sample-size formula.

\[ n \ge \frac{z_{\alpha/2}^2\,p^*(1-p^*)}{E^2} = \frac{2.326^2(0.68)(0.32)}{0.02^2} = \frac{1.1774}{0.0004} \approx 2943.5 \]

Step 4: Round up and interpret. Round up to \(n = 2944\). Sampling at least 2,944 firms keeps the error within 0.02.

A trade facilitation unit surveys 320 firms about adopting a cross-border e-invoicing system. 176 firms report that they have adopted it.

1) Construct and interpret a 99% confidence interval for the population proportion of firms that have adopted e-invoicing.

2) The unit wants a margin of error no larger than \(E = 0.01\) at 99% confidence. If no prior estimate is assumed, what minimum sample size is required?

↑

(C7) Constructs the confidence interval for a proportion.

Step 1: Identify \(x=176\), \(n=320\), and \(\hat{p} = 176/320 = 0.55\).

Step 2: Large-sample conditions.

\[ n\hat{p} = 320(0.55) = 176 \ge 10, \qquad n(1-\hat{p}) = 320(0.45) = 144 \ge 10 \]

Step 3: Standard error.

\[ SE = \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.55(0.45)}{320}} \approx 0.0278 \]

Step 4: Margin of error (99% confidence, \(z_{\alpha/2}=2.576\)).

\[ E = 2.576(0.0278) \approx 0.0716 \]

Step 5: Confidence interval.

\[ \hat{p} \pm E = 0.55 \pm 0.0716 \Rightarrow [0.478,\ 0.622] \]

Interpretation: We are 99% confident that about 47.8% to 62.2% of firms have adopted cross-border e-invoicing.

(C6) Stablishes the sample size so the error will not exceed a specified amount.

Step 1: Target error and confidence. The goal is \(E = 0.01\) at 99% confidence, so \(z_{\alpha/2}=2.576\).

Step 2: Choose \(p^*\). With no prior estimate, use the conservative choice \(p^* = 0.50\) to guarantee the margin of error.

Step 3: Apply the proportion sample-size formula.

\[ n \ge \frac{z_{\alpha/2}^2\,p^*(1-p^*)}{E^2} = \frac{2.576^2(0.50)(0.50)}{0.01^2} = \frac{1.6589}{0.0001} \approx 16589.5 \]

Step 4: Round up and interpret. Round up to \(n = 16{,}590\). A survey of at least 16,590 firms ensures the error is no more than 0.01.
