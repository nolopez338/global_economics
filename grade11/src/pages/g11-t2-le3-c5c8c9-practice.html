A ride-sharing platform studies daily gross driver payouts (hundreds of USD) in one urban zone.

A random sample of \(n=10\) days gives:

\[ 42,\ 38,\ 45,\ 40,\ 41,\ 39,\ 44,\ 43,\ 37,\ 41 \]

1) Construct a 95% confidence interval for the population mean using the t-distribution.

2) Apply the C9 structured decision procedure to compare the t-based and z-based intervals, and determine which is wider and by what percentage.

↑

(C8) t-confidence interval

\[ \mu=\text{population mean daily driver payout},\quad n=10,\quad \bar{x}=\frac{410}{10}=41.00 \]

\[ \sum (x_i-\bar{x})^2=60,\quad s=\sqrt{\frac{60}{9}}=2.582,\quad df=9 \]

\[ t^*=t_{0.025,9}\approx 2.262,\quad SE=\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{2.582}{\sqrt{10}}=0.816 \]

\[ E_t=t^*(SE)=2.262(0.816)=1.85 \]

\[ \mu\in \bar{x}\pm E_t=41.00\pm 1.85=[39.15,\,42.85] \]

(C5) z-confidence interval

\[ z^*=z_{0.025}=1.960,\qquad E_z=z^*(SE)=1.960(0.816)=1.60 \]

\[ \mu\in 41.00\pm E_z=41.00\pm 1.60=[39.40,\,42.60] \]

(C9) Structured decision procedure

\[ t^*=2.262>1.960=z^*,\qquad E_t=1.85,\ E_z=1.60 \]

\[ \text{Width}_t=2(1.85)=3.70,\qquad \text{Width}_z=2(1.60)=3.20 \]

\[ \text{Absolute difference}=3.70-3.20=0.50 \]

\[ \text{Relative difference (base }z\text{-interval)}=\frac{3.70-3.20}{3.20}\times100=15.63\% \]

Final decision: because variance is unknown and \(df=9\), the t-interval is the exact choice and is 15.63% wider than the z-interval.

An investment app analyzes mean per-trade platform fees (USD) paid by users in their 20s.

A random sample of \(n=18\) transactions gives:

\[ 1.8,\ 2.1,\ 1.9,\ 2.4,\ 2.0,\ 1.7,\ 2.2,\ 2.3,\ 1.8,\ 2.5,\ 2.1,\ 1.9,\ 2.0,\ 2.2,\ 1.6,\ 2.4,\ 2.3,\ 1.9 \]

1) Construct a 90% confidence interval using the t-distribution.

2) Apply C9 to compare t-based and z-based intervals and decide which interval to retain.

↑

(C8) t-confidence interval

\[ \mu=\text{population mean transaction fee},\quad n=18,\quad \bar{x}=\frac{37.1}{18}=2.061 \]

\[ \sum (x_i-\bar{x})^2=1.143,\quad s=\sqrt{\frac{1.143}{17}}=0.259,\quad df=17 \]

\[ t^*=t_{0.05,17}\approx 1.740,\quad SE=\frac{0.259}{\sqrt{18}}=0.0611 \]

\[ E_t=t^*(SE)=1.740(0.0611)=0.106 \]

\[ \mu\in 2.061\pm E_t=[1.955,\,2.167] \]

(C5) z-confidence interval

\[ z^*=z_{0.05}=1.645,\qquad E_z=z^*(SE)=1.645(0.0611)=0.101 \]

\[ \mu\in 2.061\pm E_z=[1.960,\,2.162] \]

(C9) Structured decision procedure

\[ t^*=1.740>1.645=z^*,\qquad E_t=0.106,\ E_z=0.101 \]

\[ \text{Width}_t=0.212,\qquad \text{Width}_z=0.202 \]

\[ \text{Absolute difference}=0.212-0.202=0.010 \]

\[ \text{Relative difference (base }z\text{-interval)}=\frac{0.212-0.202}{0.202}\times100=4.95\% \]

Final decision: keep the t-interval as the exact method under unknown variance; it is 4.95% wider.

A food-delivery app studies mean order value (USD) during weekday mornings for young professionals.

A random sample of \(n=30\) receipts gives:

\[ 115,\ 122,\ 118,\ 130,\ 126,\ 119,\ 121,\ 124,\ 128,\ 117, 123,\ 120,\ 125,\ 129,\ 116,\ 127,\ 122,\ 118,\ 124,\ 121, 126,\ 119,\ 123,\ 128,\ 120,\ 125,\ 117,\ 124,\ 122,\ 126 \]

1) Construct a 99% confidence interval with the t-distribution.

2) Compare the t and z intervals using the C9 decision sequence.

↑

(C8) t-confidence interval

\[ \mu=\text{population mean basket value},\quad n=30,\quad \bar{x}=\frac{3675}{30}=122.50 \]

\[ \sum (x_i-\bar{x})^2=477.5,\quad s=\sqrt{\frac{477.5}{29}}=4.058,\quad df=29 \]

\[ t^*=t_{0.005,29}\approx 2.756,\quad SE=\frac{4.058}{\sqrt{30}}=0.741 \]

\[ E_t=t^*(SE)=2.756(0.741)=2.04 \]

\[ \mu\in 122.50\pm E_t=[120.46,\,124.54] \]

(C5) z-confidence interval

\[ z^*=z_{0.005}=2.576,\qquad E_z=z^*(SE)=2.576(0.741)=1.91 \]

\[ \mu\in 122.50\pm E_z=[120.59,\,124.41] \]

(C9) Structured decision procedure

\[ t^*=2.756>2.576=z^*,\qquad E_t=2.04,\ E_z=1.91 \]

\[ \text{Width}_t=4.08,\qquad \text{Width}_z=3.82 \]

\[ \text{Absolute difference}=4.08-3.82=0.26 \]

\[ \text{Relative difference (base }z\text{-interval)}=\frac{4.08-3.82}{3.82}\times100=6.81\% \]

Final decision: the t-interval is still wider and remains the exact method under unknown variance.

A gig-delivery startup studies average delivery time (minutes) for same-city orders.

For \(n=60\) orders, grouped data are:

  • \(20\text{--}24\), midpoint 22, frequency 6
  • \(25\text{--}29\), midpoint 27, frequency 9
  • \(30\text{--}34\), midpoint 32, frequency 14
  • \(35\text{--}39\), midpoint 37, frequency 13
  • \(40\text{--}44\), midpoint 42, frequency 10
  • \(45\text{--}49\), midpoint 47, frequency 8

1) Construct a 95% t-confidence interval from grouped-data estimates.

2) Apply the C9 interval comparison procedure.

↑

(C8) t-confidence interval

\[ \mu=\text{population mean delivery time},\quad n=60,\quad \sum f_im_i=2100,\quad \sum f_im_i^2=77910 \]

\[ \bar{x}=\frac{2100}{60}=35.00,\quad s\approx\sqrt{\frac{77910-60(35)^2}{59}}=7.602,\quad df=59 \]

\[ t^*=t_{0.025,59}\approx2.000,\quad SE=\frac{7.602}{\sqrt{60}}=0.981 \]

\[ E_t=t^*(SE)=2.000(0.981)=1.96 \]

\[ \mu\in 35.00\pm 1.96=[33.04,\,36.96] \]

(C5) z-confidence interval

\[ z^*=1.960,\qquad E_z=z^*(SE)=1.960(0.981)=1.92 \]

\[ \mu\in 35.00\pm 1.92=[33.08,\,36.92] \]

(C9) Structured decision procedure

\[ t^*=2.000>1.960=z^*,\qquad E_t=1.96,\ E_z=1.92 \]

\[ \text{Width}_t=3.92,\qquad \text{Width}_z=3.84 \]

\[ \text{Absolute difference}=0.08,\qquad \text{Relative difference}=\frac{0.08}{3.84}\times100=2.08\% \]

Final decision: use the t-interval as exact under unknown variance; it is 2.08% wider.

A direct-to-consumer e-commerce startup estimates mean daily ad spending (thousand USD).

For \(n=120\) days, grouped data are:

  • \(50\text{--}55\), midpoint 52.5, frequency 12
  • \(55\text{--}60\), midpoint 57.5, frequency 18
  • \(60\text{--}65\), midpoint 62.5, frequency 28
  • \(65\text{--}70\), midpoint 67.5, frequency 24
  • \(70\text{--}75\), midpoint 72.5, frequency 22
  • \(75\text{--}80\), midpoint 77.5, frequency 16

1) Construct a 90% t-confidence interval for the population mean using grouped-data estimates.

2) Apply the C9 comparison process against the z-based interval.

↑

(C8) t-confidence interval

\[ \mu=\text{population mean daily ad spending},\quad n=120,\quad \sum f_im_i=7870,\quad \sum f_im_i^2=524975 \]

\[ \bar{x}=\frac{7870}{120}=65.583,\quad s\approx\sqrt{\frac{524975-120(65.583)^2}{119}}=7.620,\quad df=119 \]

\[ t^*=t_{0.05,119}\approx1.658,\quad SE=\frac{7.620}{\sqrt{120}}=0.696 \]

\[ E_t=t^*(SE)=1.658(0.696)=1.15 \]

\[ \mu\in 65.583\pm1.15=[64.43,\,66.74] \]

(C5) z-confidence interval

\[ z^*=z_{0.05}=1.645,\qquad E_z=z^*(SE)=1.645(0.696)=1.14 \]

\[ \mu\in 65.583\pm1.14=[64.44,\,66.72] \]

(C9) Structured decision procedure

\[ t^*=1.658>1.645=z^*,\qquad E_t=1.15,\ E_z=1.14 \]

\[ \text{Width}_t=2.30,\qquad \text{Width}_z=2.28 \]

\[ \text{Absolute difference}=0.02,\qquad \text{Relative difference}=\frac{0.02}{2.28}\times100=0.88\% \]

Final decision: t and z are very close at this sample size, but the t-interval is still the exact and slightly wider interval.
