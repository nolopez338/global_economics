A benchmark claim concerns a population mean \(\mu\). A random sample of size \(n\) has sample mean \(\bar x\), and the population standard deviation \(\sigma\) is known. At significance level \(\alpha\), test whether the true mean is lower than, higher than, or different from the benchmark \(\mu_0\).

Questions

  1. Identify the population parameter and write hypotheses for lower-tail, higher-tail, and two-tailed alternatives.
  2. Write the one-sample mean \(z\)-test statistic.
  3. State how p-values are computed for each tail type.
  4. State the decision rule using p-value and \(\alpha\).
  5. Write a contextual conclusion template for reject/fail-to-reject outcomes.
  • Parameter: \(\mu\) = true population mean in context.
  • Hypotheses: \(H_0\colon \mu=\mu_0\), \(H_a\colon \mu<\mu_0\) or \(\mu>\mu_0\) or \(\mu\ne\mu_0\).
  • Test statistic (known \(\sigma\)): \(z=\frac{\bar x-\mu_0}{\sigma/\sqrt{n}}\).
  • p-value by tail type:
    • Lower-tail: \(P(Z\le z_{obs})\)
    • Higher-tail: \(P(Z\ge z_{obs})\)
    • Two-tailed: \(2P(Z\ge |z_{obs}|)\)
  • Decision rule: Reject \(H_0\) if p-value \(\le\alpha\); otherwise fail to reject \(H_0\).
  • Conclusion template: “There is sufficient evidence” (reject \(H_0\)) or “There is not enough evidence” (fail to reject \(H_0\)) for the contextual claim in \(H_a\).

A company benchmark for average commute time is 42 minutes. After a schedule redesign, a random sample of 100 employees has mean commute time 41.0 minutes. The population standard deviation is known to be 5 minutes. At \(\alpha=5\%\), test whether the true mean commute time is lower than 42 minutes.

  • Hypotheses: \(H_0\colon \mu=42\), \(H_a\colon \mu<42\).
  • Test statistic: \(z=\frac{41.0-42}{5/\sqrt{100}}=\frac{-1.0}{0.5}=-2.00\).
  • p-value: \(P(Z\le -2.00)\approx 0.0228\).
  • Decision: Reject \(H_0\) since \(0.0228<0.05\).
  • Conclusion: There is sufficient evidence that the true mean commute time is below 42 minutes.

A provider benchmark is 45 dollars per month for average phone-plan spending. A random sample of 64 customers has mean spending 46.2 dollars. The known population standard deviation is 8 dollars. At \(\alpha=5\%\), test whether the true mean spending is higher than 45 dollars.

  • Hypotheses: \(H_0\colon \mu=45\), \(H_a\colon \mu>45\).
  • Test statistic: \(z=\frac{46.2-45}{8/\sqrt{64}}=1.20\).
  • p-value: \(P(Z\ge 1.20)\approx 0.1151\).
  • Decision: Fail to reject \(H_0\) since \(0.1151>0.05\).
  • Conclusion: There is not enough evidence that the true mean spending exceeds 45 dollars.
