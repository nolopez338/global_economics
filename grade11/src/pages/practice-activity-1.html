Solution: Problem 0

Problem restatement

A small retail store recorded the checkout times (in seconds) of a random sample of 10 customers.

The recorded times were:

\[42,\ 38,\ 51,\ 47,\ 44,\ 39,\ 56,\ 48,\ 41,\ 52.\]

The manager wants to estimate the population mean checkout time and quantify confidence in that estimate.

1. Sample Statistics

Let the data be \(x_1, x_2, \dots, x_{10}\).

\[ \bar{x} = \frac{42 + 38 + 51 + 47 + 44 + 39 + 56 + 48 + 41 + 52}{10} = \frac{458}{10} = 45.8 \text{ seconds} \]

\[s \approx 6.23 \text{ seconds}\]

2. Standard Error

\[ SE = \frac{s}{\sqrt{10}} = \frac{6.23}{3.162} \approx 1.97 \]

3. Distribution Choice

Sample size \(n = 10 < 30\) and population variance unknown, so it is appropriate to use the t-distribution with 9 degrees of freedom.

4. 95% Confidence Interval

Using \(t_{0.025,9} \approx 2.262\):

\[\mu \in 45.8 \pm 2.262(1.97)\]

\[\mu \in [41.34,\ 50.26] \text{ seconds}\]

5. Interpretation

The store can be 95% confident that the true average checkout time lies between approximately 41 and 50 seconds.

Solution: Problem 1

Problem restatement

A café tracks the number of daily online orders for a random sample of 50 days.

The data were recorded in grouped form:

Order Count Frequency Total Contribution
51010(5) = 50
91515(9) = 135
121818(12) = 216
2077(20) = 140

The owner wants to estimate the population mean number of daily orders and produce a 95% confidence interval.

1. Sample Mean

\[ \bar{x} = \frac{50 + 135 + 216 + 140}{50} = \frac{541}{50} = 10.82 \]

2. Sample Variance (Grouped)

\[ s^2 = \frac{1}{n-1}\sum f(x - \bar{x})^2 \]

Compute contributions:

\[ \begin{aligned} 10(5 - 10.82)^2 &= 339.2 \\ 15(9 - 10.82)^2 &= 49.8 \\ 18(12 - 10.82)^2 &= 24.4 \\ 7(20 - 10.82)^2 &= 604.0 \end{aligned} \]

\[ \sum f(x - \bar{x})^2 = 1017.4 \]

\[ s^2 = \frac{1017.4}{49} = 20.77,\qquad s = 4.56 \]

3. Standard Error

\[ SE = \frac{s}{\sqrt{50}} = \frac{4.56}{7.07} \approx 0.65 \]

4. Confidence Interval (t, 49 df)

\[ \mu \in 10.82 \pm 2.009(0.65) \]

\[ \mu \in [9.51,\ 12.13] \]

5. Interpretation

The café can be 95% confident that the true average number of daily online orders lies between about 9.5 and 12.1.

Solution: Problem 2

Problem restatement

An online boutique records weekly revenue (in thousand USD) from a grouped random sample of 40 weeks.

The data are:

Revenue (k USD) Frequency Contribution
458360
5012600
5514770
806480

The boutique wants a 95% confidence interval for the population mean weekly revenue.

1. Sample Mean

\[ \bar{x} = \frac{360 + 600 + 770 + 480}{40} = \frac{2210}{40} = 55.25 \]

2. Sample Variance

Compute deviations:

\[ \begin{aligned} 8(45 - 55.25)^2 &= 8(105.6) = 844.8 \\ 12(50 - 55.25)^2 &= 12(27.6) = 331.2 \\ 14(55 - 55.25)^2 &= 14(0.0625) = 0.875 \\ 6(80 - 55.25)^2 &= 6(612.56) = 3675.4 \end{aligned} \]

\[ \sum f(x - \bar{x})^2 = 4852.3 \]

\[ s^2 = \frac{4852.3}{39} = 124.43,\qquad s = 11.15 \]

3. Standard Error

\[ SE = \frac{11.15}{\sqrt{40}} = 1.76 \]

4. Confidence Interval (t, 39 df)

\[ \mu \in 55.25 \pm 2.023(1.76) \]

\[ \mu \in [51.69,\ 58.81] \]

5. Interpretation

The boutique can be 95% confident the true average weekly revenue is between about 51.7k and 58.8k USD.

Solution: Problem 3

Problem restatement

A logistics startup studies processing costs (USD) using a grouped random sample of 60 shipments.

Due to extreme variation across global hubs, the grouped data include low, mid, and extreme high-cost categories:

Cost (USD) Frequency Contribution
70181260
95201900
120121440
250102500

Construct a point estimate, compute the sample standard deviation using grouped formulas, justify the appropriate distribution for heavy-tailed data, and produce a 95% confidence interval.

1. Sample Mean

\[ \bar{x} = \frac{1260 + 1900 + 1440 + 2500}{60} = \frac{7100}{60} = 118.33 \]

2. Sample Variance

Compute deviations:

\[ \begin{aligned} 18(70 - 118.33)^2 &= 18(2320.6) = 41770.8 \\ 20(95 - 118.33)^2 &= 20(542.1) = 10842 \\ 12(120 - 118.33)^2 &= 12(2.78) = 33.36 \\ 10(250 - 118.33)^2 &= 10(17381.5) = 173815 \end{aligned} \]

\[ \sum f(x - \bar{x})^2 = 226461.2 \]

\[ s^2 = \frac{226461.2}{59} = 3830.69,\qquad s = 61.90 \]

3. Standard Error

\[ SE = \frac{61.90}{\sqrt{60}} = 7.98 \]

4. Distribution Choice

Even though \(n = 60\), the presence of heavy-tailed behavior justifies using a t-distribution.

5. 95% Confidence Interval (t, 59 df)

\[ \mu \in 118.33 \pm 2.000(7.98) \]

\[ \mu \in [102.37,\ 134.29] \]

6. Interpretation

The true mean processing cost likely lies between 102 and 134 USD, but the wide interval reflects high volatility and the large influence of extreme high-cost hubs.
