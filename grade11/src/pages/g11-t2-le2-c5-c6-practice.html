An inflation monitoring team wants to estimate the mean price of a staple food basket (in dollars) across markets. They know the population standard deviation is \(\sigma = 0.60\) dollars and the current sample mean is \(\bar{x} = 2.85\) dollars. The team has collected \(n_{\text{current}} = 36\) market prices and wants a 90% confidence interval. (a) Construct the confidence interval for the mean price. (b) How many additional observations are required to reduce the margin of error to \(E = 0.12\) dollars?

↑

This solution calculates the confidence interval for the mean with known variance using the current sample information.

Step 1: Compute the standard error.

\[ SE = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n_{\text{current}}}} = \frac{0.60}{\sqrt{36}} = 0.10 \]

Step 2: Compute the margin of error. For 90% confidence, \(z^* = 1.645\).

\[ E = z^* \cdot \left(\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}\right) \]

\[ E = 1.645 \cdot \left(\frac{0.60}{\sqrt{36}}\right) = 0.1645 \approx 0.16 \]

Step 3: Construct the confidence interval.

\[ \bar{x} \pm E = 2.85 \pm 0.16 = (2.69,\ 3.01) \]

Conclusion. The 90% confidence interval is \((2.69,\ 3.01)\) dollars.

↑

This subsection establishes the required sample size so the margin of error does not exceed the target amount.

Step 1: Use the target error and confidence level. The target margin of error is \(E = 0.12\) dollars and for 90% confidence, \(z^* = 1.645\).

Step 2: Compute the required sample size.

\[ n_{\text{required}} = \left(\frac{z^*\,\sigma}{E}\right)^2 = \left(\frac{1.645 \cdot 0.60}{0.12}\right)^2 = \left(8.225\right)^2 \approx 67.65 \]

Step 3: Determine additional observations. Round up to \(n_{\text{required}} = 68\). The additional observations needed are \(68 - 36 = 32\).

Conclusion. The team needs 32 more observations so the error will not exceed 0.12 dollars.

A distribution center tracks the mean time (in minutes) required to handle incoming shipments. The population standard deviation is known to be \(\sigma = 12\) minutes. The current sample mean is \(\bar{x} = 52\) minutes with \(n_{\text{current}} = 40\) observations and a 95% confidence level. (a) Construct the confidence interval for the mean handling time. (b) How many additional observations are needed to reach a target interval width of \(W = 6\) minutes? (Recall \(W = \text{Upper} - \text{Lower} = 2E\).)

↑

This solution calculates a confidence interval with known variance using the current sample information.

Step 1: Compute the standard error.

\[ SE = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n_{\text{current}}}} = \frac{12}{\sqrt{40}} \approx 1.90 \]

Step 2: Compute the margin of error. For 95% confidence, \(z^* = 1.96\).

\[ E = z^* \cdot \left(\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}\right) \]

\[ E = 1.96 \cdot \left(\frac{12}{\sqrt{40}}\right) \approx 3.72 \]

Step 3: Construct the confidence interval.

\[ \bar{x} \pm E = 52 \pm 3.72 = (48.28,\ 55.72) \]

Conclusion. The 95% confidence interval is \((48.28,\ 55.72)\) minutes.

↑

This subsection establishes the required sample size so the interval width stays within the target amount.

Step 1: Convert width to margin of error. The target width is \(W = 6\), so \(E = W/2 = 3\).

Step 2: Compute the required sample size. For 95% confidence, \(z^* = 1.96\).

\[ n_{\text{required}} = \left(\frac{z^*\,\sigma}{E}\right)^2 = \left(\frac{1.96 \cdot 12}{3}\right)^2 = \left(7.84\right)^2 \approx 61.47 \]

Step 3: Determine additional observations. Round up to \(n_{\text{required}} = 62\). The additional observations needed are \(62 - 40 = 22\).

Conclusion. The distribution center needs 22 more observations so the error will not exceed 3 minutes (width 6 minutes).

A microcredit program tracks the mean processing fee (in dollars) charged per loan. The population standard deviation is known to be \(\sigma = 16\) dollars. The current sample mean is \(\bar{x} = 88\) dollars with \(n_{\text{current}} = 64\) observations at 90% confidence. (a) Construct the confidence interval for the mean fee. (b) How many observations are required so that the new margin of error is the current margin of error minus 1.0 dollar? (Use \(E_{\text{new}} = E_{\text{current}} - 1.0\).)

↑

This solution computes the confidence interval for the mean with known variance using the current sample information.

Step 1: Compute the standard error.

\[ SE = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n_{\text{current}}}} = \frac{16}{\sqrt{64}} = 2 \]

Step 2: Compute the margin of error. For 90% confidence, \(z^* = 1.645\).

\[ E_{\text{current}} = z^* SE = 1.645(2) = 3.29 \]

Step 3: Construct the confidence interval.

\[ \bar{x} \pm E_{\text{current}} = 88 \pm 3.29 = (84.71,\ 91.29) \]

Conclusion. The 90% confidence interval is \((84.71,\ 91.29)\).

↑

Using this confidence-interval result, determine the sample size needed to reduce the margin of error.

Compute the new target margin of error. The new target is defined by \(E_{\text{new}} = E_{\text{current}} - 1.0\).

\[ E_{\text{new}} = E_{\text{current}} - 1.0 = 3.29 - 1.0 = 2.29 \]

Compute the required sample size.

\[ n_{\text{required}} = \left(\frac{z^*\,\sigma}{E_{\text{new}}}\right)^2 = \left(\frac{1.645 \cdot 16}{2.29}\right)^2 = \left(11.50\right)^2 \approx 132.25 \]

Determine additional observations. Round up to \(n_{\text{required}} = 133\).

\[ n_{\text{additional}} = n_{\text{required}} - n_{\text{current}} = 133 - 64 = 69 \]

Conclusion. A total of 133 observations are required to reduce the margin of error by 1.0 dollar.

The current sample mean is \(\bar{x} = 205\) liters with \(n_{\text{current}} = 81\) observations at 95% confidence. (a) Construct the confidence interval for the mean delivery volume. (b) How many observations are required so that the new margin of error is the current margin of error minus 1.0 liter? (Use \(E_{\text{new}} = E_{\text{current}} - 1.0\).)

↑

This solution computes the confidence interval for the mean with known variance using the current sample information.

Step 1: Compute the standard error.

\[ SE = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n_{\text{current}}}} = \frac{18}{\sqrt{81}} = 2 \]

Step 2: Compute the margin of error. For 95% confidence, \(z^* = 1.96\).

\[ E_{\text{current}} = z^* SE = 1.96(2) = 3.92 \]

Step 3: Construct the confidence interval.

\[ \bar{x} \pm E_{\text{current}} = 205 \pm 3.92 = (201.08,\ 208.92) \]

Conclusion. The 95% confidence interval is \((201.08,\ 208.92)\).

↑

Using this confidence-interval result, determine the sample size needed to reduce the margin of error.

Compute the new target margin of error. The new target is defined by \(E_{\text{new}} = E_{\text{current}} - 1.0\).

\[ E_{\text{new}} = E_{\text{current}} - 1.0 = 3.92 - 1.0 = 2.92 \]

Compute the required sample size.

\[ n_{\text{required}} = \left(\frac{z^*\,\sigma}{E_{\text{new}}}\right)^2 = \left(\frac{1.96 \cdot 18}{2.92}\right)^2 = \left(12.08\right)^2 \approx 145.93 \]

Determine additional observations. Round up to \(n_{\text{required}} = 146\).

\[ n_{\text{additional}} = n_{\text{required}} - n_{\text{current}} = 146 - 81 = 65 \]

Conclusion. A total of 146 observations are required to reduce the margin of error by 1.0 liter.

An export office tracks the mean documentation cost (in dollars) per shipment. The population standard deviation is \(\sigma = 21\) dollars. The current sample mean is \(\bar{x} = 310\) dollars with \(n_{\text{current}} = 49\) observations at 98% confidence. (a) Construct the confidence interval for the mean documentation cost. (b) How many observations are required so that the new margin of error is the current margin of error minus 1.5 dollars? (Use \(E_{\text{new}} = E_{\text{current}} - 1.5\).)

↑

This solution computes the confidence interval for the mean with known variance using the current sample information.

Step 1: Compute the standard error.

\[ SE = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n_{\text{current}}}} = \frac{21}{\sqrt{49}} = 3 \]

Step 2: Compute the margin of error. For 98% confidence, \(z^* = 2.326\).

\[ E_{\text{current}} = z^* SE = 2.326(3) = 6.978 \approx 6.98 \]

Step 3: Construct the confidence interval.

\[ \bar{x} \pm E_{\text{current}} = 310 \pm 6.98 = (303.02,\ 316.98) \]

Conclusion. The 98% confidence interval is \((303.02,\ 316.98)\).

↑

Using this confidence-interval result, determine the sample size needed to reduce the margin of error.

Compute the new target margin of error. The new target is defined by \(E_{\text{new}} = E_{\text{current}} - 1.5\).

\[ E_{\text{new}} = E_{\text{current}} - 1.5 = 6.98 - 1.5 = 5.48 \]

Compute the required sample size.

\[ n_{\text{required}} = \left(\frac{z^*\,\sigma}{E_{\text{new}}}\right)^2 = \left(\frac{2.326 \cdot 21}{5.48}\right)^2 = \left(8.91\right)^2 \approx 79.40 \]

Determine additional observations. Round up to \(n_{\text{required}} = 80\).

\[ n_{\text{additional}} = n_{\text{required}} - n_{\text{current}} = 80 - 49 = 31 \]

Conclusion. A total of 80 observations are required to reduce the margin of error by 1.5 dollars.
