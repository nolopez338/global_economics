Conceptual Referents

  • Area of simple geometric figures.
  • Sample and population
  • Random variable
  • Continuous probability distribution
  • Mean and variance.
  • Normal distribution
  • t-student distribution
  • Investment portfolios

Inference in Context

  • Confidence statements: Frame findings in economic language (e.g., "We are 95% confident the average return lies between...").
  • Normal vs. t-student: Choose based on whether variance is known and sample size.
  • Proportion focus: Useful for adoption rates, default rates, or market share analyses.

Portfolio Applications

Example: Estimating the mean return of a portfolio with unknown variance uses t critical values; the resulting interval guides whether to rebalance toward safer assets.

Interpret intervals alongside risk measures to decide if expected performance meets economic objectives.

Confidence & Interval Estimation Formulas.

Confidence & Interval Estimation Formulas is a Grade 11 reference page that summarizes core estimation formulas across eight sections.

  • Sample mean
  • Sample and population variance / standard deviation
  • Bernoulli mean and variance
  • Margin of error for a mean (z and t)
  • Confidence interval for a mean
  • Section 1 — Getting Oriented: Random Variables and Notation: Notation for samples, observations, and variables.
  • Section 2 — General formulas: Dataset setup and notation reminders.
  • Section 3 — Point Estimates: Sample mean and proportion formulas.
  • Section 4 — Variability: Sample/population variance and Bernoulli variance.
  • Section 5 — Standard Error: Standard error for proportions.
  • Section 6 — Margin of Error: Margin of error for mean and proportion.
  • Section 7 — Confidence Intervals: Interval formulas for mean and proportion.
  • Section 8 — Sample Size for Target Margin of Error: Mean and proportion sample size planning.
