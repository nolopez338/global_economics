\section{G10\_T1\_L1\_C1-C2\_practice\_activity1}
This practice activity develops the first steps of the decision-analysis learning ladder by having students identify decision alternatives, uncertain events, states of nature, and consequences before arranging the same information in payoff tables. Across the problems, students move from direct interpretation of given alternatives and states to organizing two-way and multi-state payoff structures, including cases where payoffs must be calculated from quantities, unit profits, and fixed adjustments. The focus remains on (C1) interpreting decision alternatives, events, consequences, and states and (C2) building the payoff table.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Local Café Investment:} Students begin the ladder by distinguishing the owner's two menu alternatives from the high- and low-traffic states, then matching each alternative--state pair to a profit entry in a payoff table.
    \item \textbf{Green Energy Production Mix:} Students extend C1 interpretation to three generation strategies and three weather states, then practice organizing the full set of strategy--weather consequences into a payoff table.
    \item \textbf{Global Shipping Network Design:} Students connect long-term network choices to several possible trade conditions, using the expanded state space to build a payoff table that includes both gains and losses.
    \item \textbf{Community Bakery Lunch Boxes:} Students identify packaging choices and demand states, then develop the C2 step by converting expected lunch-box quantities and per-box profits into payoff-table entries.
    \item \textbf{Rural Internet Subscriptions:} Students classify subscription-plan alternatives and enrollment states, then translate subscription counts and per-subscription profits into an organized payoff table.
    \item \textbf{Coffee Export Routes:} Students interpret route choices under exchange-rate uncertainty and build the payoff table by connecting the realized rate to shipments and profit per shipment.
    \item \textbf{Lake Ferry Tourism:} Students separate ferry-size alternatives from seasonal tourism states, then construct payoff entries by combining ticket quantities with season-dependent profit per ticket.
    \item \textbf{Cold-Storage Contracts:} Students move from identifying facility alternatives and demand states to building payoffs that combine stored-pallet quantities, per-pallet profit, and fixed facility profit.
    \item \textbf{Solar Installation Bids:} Students interpret installation-focus alternatives and policy states, then organize payoff-table consequences that account for installations, per-installation profit, and the policy-based fixed adjustment.
    \item \textbf{Air Cargo Charters:} Students connect aircraft-lease alternatives to fuel-price states and build the payoff table by combining charter volumes, fuel-dependent profit per charter, and fixed lease profit.
    \item \textbf{Seafood Export Licenses:} Students complete the ladder by identifying export-focus alternatives and seasonal states, then organizing crate-based profits and seasonal fixed adjustments into the payoff table.
\end{enumerate}
