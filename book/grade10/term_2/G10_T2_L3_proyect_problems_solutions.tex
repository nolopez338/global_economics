		A coffee-export startup must choose one logistics model for the next season:
		$A_1$ (outsourced shipping), $A_2$ (mixed model), $A_3$ (own fleet), $A_4$ (regional consolidation hubs), or $A_5$ (strategic alliance network).
		The team estimates three demand states:
		\[
		H:0.45,\quad M:0.35,\quad L:0.20.
		\]
		Payoffs are in thousand USD.
		After the first decision, international freight regulation may change, so management wants a second-stage decision tree to verify if the C8 recommendation remains robust under this extra uncertainty.
		After obtaining the C10 summary and computing the C8 results, apply the following structured C9 decision procedure:
		\begin{itemize}
			\item Retain the top four alternatives according to EV (4 alternatives remain).
			\item From that subset, eliminate the alternative with the highest Max regret (3 alternatives remain).
			\item Select the alternative with the highest minimum payoff (Maximin) inside the reduced subset of three (final decision).
		\end{itemize}
