		\subsection*{Problem 1 — Homework completion time app}

		\textbf{Problem.}
		A school introduces a planning app and claims that the average time students need to finish nightly homework is now above 90 minutes. A random sample of Grade 11 students records daily homework time. The parameter of interest is the population mean homework time \(\mu\), and the claim motivates a formal test.

		\textbf{Question.}
		(a) C1: State the statistical hypothesis idea in words for this scenario.
		(b) C2: Write \(H_0\) and \(H_a\) using proper parameter notation.
		(c) C3: Decide one-tailed vs two-tailed and justify in one sentence.

		\textbf{Solution.}
		\textbf{C1.} We test a statement about the population mean \(\mu\) using sample homework times from students. The sample gives evidence about whether the true average homework time is really above the benchmark value.
		\textbf{C2.} \(H_0\colon \mu = 90\) minutes and \(H_a\colon \mu > 90\) minutes.
		\textbf{C3.} Right-tailed test. The claim uses above 90 minutes, so only larger values of \(\mu\) support the claim.
