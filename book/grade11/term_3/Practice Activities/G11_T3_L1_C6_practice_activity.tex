\begin{ExamProblems}
\item
\textbf{Statement.} Interprets decisions from hypothesis tests in terms of the context of the problem.

\textbf{Problem.} A company claims that the mean volume of juice bottles is \(\mu_0=600\) mL. A hypothesis test is performed at the 5\% significance level, and the final decision is to reject \(H_0\). Explain what this decision means in context and what cannot be concluded.

\textbf{Solution.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Meaning of reject \(H_0\):} At the 5\% significance level, the sample provides sufficient evidence that the population mean bottle volume is different from 600 mL (or in the claimed direction, if a one-tailed test was used).

    \item \textbf{What cannot be concluded:}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item We cannot conclude with certainty that the mean is not 600 mL; decisions are probabilistic.
        \item We cannot conclude that every single bottle is below or above 600 mL.
        \item The conclusion is about the population mean, not each individual bottle.
    \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}
\end{ExamProblems}
