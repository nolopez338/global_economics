\SubsectionBox{Criteria assessment}\vspace{-0.25cm}
Each assessment criterion is evaluated across the worked examples and activity questions. A criterion is considered passed when it is correctly activated in 4 of the 6 assigned tasks.

For work to count as a valid completed set of tasks, the tasks must include:
\begin{itemize}
	\item exactly half proportion/probability settings and exactly half mean settings, so that Criterion C1 can be validly assessed;
	\item at least 1 left-tailed setup (lower than), at least 1 right-tailed setup (higher than), and at least 1 two-tailed setup (different from), so that Criteria C2 and C3 can be validly assessed.
\end{itemize}


\vspace{0.25cm}
\SubsectionBox{Student Problems}\vspace{-0.25cm}

Use the following shared instructions for all six problems:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(C1)] Describe the context; state whether it is a population mean or population proportion setting; define the parameter; explain how you would collect sample information; and invent a plausible sample result.
\item[(C2)] State the benchmark claim and justify it narratively; choose and justify a significance level; then write \(H_0\) and \(H_a\) with correct notation, keeping the null as ``parameter equals benchmark.''
\item[(C3)] Classify your test as left-tailed, right-tailed, or two-tailed, and justify the classification from the wording and intention of your claim.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Problem 1 --- School context}\\
Create a hypothesis test about a measurable feature of your school.

\item \textbf{Problem 2 --- Home context}\\
Create a hypothesis test about a measurable feature of life at home.

\item \textbf{Problem 3 --- Neighborhood context}\\
Create a hypothesis test about a measurable feature of your neighborhood.

\item \textbf{Problem 4 --- Your Grade 11 group context}\\
Create a hypothesis test about a measurable feature of your own Grade 11 group.

\item \textbf{Problem 5 --- Personal context}\\
Create a hypothesis test about a measurable feature related to yourself.

\item \textbf{Problem 6 --- Bogotá context}\\
Create a hypothesis test about a measurable feature of Bogotá that can be investigated at your level.
\end{enumerate}
