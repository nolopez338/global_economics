Formulas Used in This Topic

Reference formulas for payoff tables, decision criteria, and expected value.

On this page

  • Decision-making notation
  • Expected monetary value (EMV)
  • Maximin criterion
  • Maximax criterion
  • Maximum opportunity criterion (minimax regret)
↑ Back to Top

The formulas below use a payoff table with decisions and uncertain future states. Keep the following notation in mind.

  • \(A_j\): a decision alternative (strategy) for \(j = 1, 2, \dots, n\).
  • \(S_i\): a state of nature (future scenario) for \(i = 1, 2, \dots, m\).
  • \(\text{Payoff}(A_j,S_i)\): outcome (profit or loss) if \(A_j\) happens and \(S_i\) occurs.
  • \(p_i\): probability of state \(S_i\) (only used when probabilities are given).
  • \(\max_i\) / \(\min_i\): pick the best or worst payoff within a row.
  • \(\text{Regret}(A_j,S_i)\): missed opportunity compared with the best payoff in state \(S_i\).
↑

\[ EMV(A_j) = \sum_{i=1}^{m} p_i \,\text{Payoff}(A_j,S_i) \]

Where:

  • \(A_j\): decision alternative (e.g., a strategy).
  • \(S_i\): state of nature (future scenario).
  • \(\text{Payoff}(A_j,S_i)\): monetary result of \(A_j\) under state \(S_i\).
  • \(p_i\): probability of state \(S_i\).
  • \(m\): number of states of nature.

Context: Represents the average monetary performance of a strategy under uncertainty.

↑

\[ \text{Maximin}(A_j) = \max_j \left( \min_i \,\text{Payoff}(A_j,S_i) \right) \]

Where:

  • \(A_j\): decision alternatives.
  • \(S_i\): states of nature.
  • \(\min_i \text{Payoff}(A_j,S_i)\): worst-case payoff of \(A_j\).
  • Outer max selects the highest worst-case result.

Context: Represents a conservative decision maker seeking safety.

↑

\[ \text{Maximax}(A_j) = \max_j \left( \max_i \,\text{Payoff}(A_j,S_i) \right) \]

Where:

  • Inner max: best payoff each alternative can achieve.
  • Outer max: selects the alternative with the largest best-case payoff.

Context: Represents an optimistic decision maker seeking maximum possible gain.

↑

\[ \text{Regret}(A_j,S_i) = \max_j \text{Payoff}(A_j,S_i) - \text{Payoff}(A_j,S_i) \]

\[ \text{Minimax Regret} = \min_j \left( \max_i \,\text{Regret}(A_j,S_i) \right) \]

Where:

  • \(A_j\): decision alternatives.
  • \(S_i\): states of nature.
  • \(\max_j \text{Payoff}(A_j,S_i)\): best payoff available in state \(S_i\).
  • \(\max_i \text{Regret}(A_j,S_i)\): largest regret for alternative \(A_j\).

Context: Chooses the alternative that minimizes the worst missed opportunity.
