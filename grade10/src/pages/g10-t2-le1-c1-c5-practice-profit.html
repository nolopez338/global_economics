Narrative problem description

A small café has budget for only one menu strategy for the coming season, so the owner must choose either launching the artisan dessert line or keeping the current menu. Foot-traffic uncertainty is summarized by two states: high traffic and low traffic, based on last year’s daily counts and a neighborhood survey that estimate a 0.55 probability of high traffic and a 0.45 probability of low traffic. The finance team estimates net profit (in thousand USD) of 42 when the dessert line meets high traffic but only 8 when traffic is low because of added fixed baking costs, while keeping the current menu yields 28 in high traffic and 20 in low traffic due to steadier costs but fewer upsell opportunities.


Question: Make a decision on the best option based on three perspectives: safe behavior (Maximin), balanced behavior (Expected Value), and risky behavior (Maximax).

Summary description

  • Economic context and transaction: daily café sales with profit per dessert plate sold.
  • Decision alternatives: launch the dessert line; keep the current menu.
  • States of nature: high traffic; low traffic.
  • State probabilities: high traffic → 0.55, low traffic → 0.45.
  • Payoffs defined as: profit in thousand USD: launch dessert line → 42 (high), 8 (low); keep menu → 28 (high), 20 (low).

The maximax rule selects the alternative with the highest possible payoff. \[ \begin{aligned} \max_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Launch}, S_i) &= \max\{42, 8\} = 42 \\ \max_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Keep}, S_i) &= \max\{28, 20\} = 28 \end{aligned} \] \[ \max\{42, 28\} = 42 \] Because 42 is the largest best payoff, the maximax (risk-seeking) recommendation is launch the dessert line.
↑
The maximin rule focuses on the worst-case payoff of each alternative. \[ \begin{aligned} \min_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Launch}, S_i) &= \min\{42, 8\} = 8 \\ \min_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Keep}, S_i) &= \min\{28, 20\} = 20 \end{aligned} \] \[ \max\{8, 20\} = 20 \] Since 20 exceeds 8, the maximin (risk-averse) choice is keep the current menu.
↑
We choose the option that maximizes the café's profit objective. Alternatives are launch or keep the menu; states are high or low traffic. Expected values: \[ \begin{aligned} EV_{\text{Launch}} &= 0.55(42) + 0.45(8) = 23.1 + 3.6 = 26.7 \\ EV_{\text{Keep}} &= 0.55(28) + 0.45(20) = 15.4 + 9 = 24.4 \end{aligned} \] \[ \max\{26.7, 24.4\} = 26.7 \] Summary of recommendations across decision criteria
  • Maximax favors launching the dessert line (highest payoff of 42).
  • Maximin favors keeping the current menu (best worst-case payoff of 20).
  • Expected value favors launching the dessert line (EV of 26.7).
Launching desserts leads on optimistic and average outcomes, while the current menu protects the downside.

Narrative problem description

A renewable-energy company must lock in one generation mix because its annual capital budget cannot cover multiple builds, so the realistic alternatives are wind turbines, solar farms, or a balanced hybrid system. Weather is the key uncertainty and is modeled with windy, sunny, and cloudy states using historical meteorological records that assign probabilities of 0.30, 0.45, and 0.25, respectively. Estimated net profit (in thousand USD) is 90, 45, and 30 for wind turbines under windy, sunny, and cloudy conditions; 35, 95, and 25 for solar farms; and 70, 65, and 60 for the hybrid, reflecting that wind excels in windy years, solar peaks in sunny years, and the hybrid smooths output but never dominates extremes.


Question: Make a decision on the best strategy based on three perspectives: safe behavior (Maximin), balanced behavior (Expected Value), and risky behavior (Maximax).

Summary description

  • Economic context and transaction: annual electricity sales with profit per megawatt-hour generated and sold.
  • Decision alternatives: build wind turbines; build solar farms; build a balanced hybrid system.
  • States of nature: windy; sunny; cloudy.
  • State probabilities: windy → 0.30, sunny → 0.45, cloudy → 0.25.
  • Payoffs defined as: profit in thousand USD: wind turbines → 90 (windy), 45 (sunny), 30 (cloudy); solar farms → 35 (windy), 95 (sunny), 25 (cloudy); hybrid system → 70 (windy), 65 (sunny), 60 (cloudy).

Narrative problem description

Question: Compute profits and choose the best option using Maximin, Expected Value, and Maximax.

Summary description

  • State probabilities: high demand → 0.60, low demand → 0.40.

Narrative problem description

A logistics firm must select a single warehouse size—small, medium, or large—because financing allows only one facility choice. Storage demand is uncertain with high and low states assigned probabilities of 0.55 and 0.45 from customer contract projections. The profit model includes fixed components of 40, 25, and 10 thousand USD for small, medium, and large facilities plus per-pallet profits of 0.18, 0.12, and 0.08 thousand USD, and the expected pallets stored (the payoffs in the table) are 300 and 220 for small, 600 and 420 for medium, and 900 and 700 for large, showing how higher fixed gains for small facilities trade off against higher volumes for larger sites.


Question: Compute profits and choose the best facility using Maximin, Expected Value, and Maximax.

Summary description

  • Economic context and transaction: storage contracts with profit per pallet stored plus a fixed profit tied to the facility choice.
  • Decision alternatives: small facility (fixed profit 40 thousand USD, 0.18 thousand USD per pallet); medium facility (fixed profit 25 thousand USD, 0.12 thousand USD per pallet); large facility (fixed profit 10 thousand USD, 0.08 thousand USD per pallet).
