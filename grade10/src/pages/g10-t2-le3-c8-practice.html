A manufacturing cooperative must choose one production plan for the next month: Plan \(A_1\), Plan \(A_2\), or Plan \(A_3\). There are six market-cost states with probabilities:

\[ S_1:0.18,\ S_2:0.17,\ S_3:0.16,\ S_4:0.15,\ S_5:0.19,\ S_6:0.15, \]

which sum to \(1.00\). Payoffs are in thousand USD. Apply Bayes (EV), Maximin, and Minimax Regret.

State of natureProbability\(A_1\)\(A_2\)\(A_3\)
\(S_1\)0.18120110140
\(S_2\)0.179510090
\(S_3\)0.16808570
\(S_4\)0.15607065
\(S_5\)0.19405535
\(S_6\)0.15203025
↑

Structured solution

Step 1. Build the payoff table.

Payoff table

State of natureProbability\(A_1\)\(A_2\)\(A_3\)
\(S_1\)0.18120110140
\(S_2\)0.179510090
\(S_3\)0.16808570
\(S_4\)0.15607065
\(S_5\)0.19405535
\(S_6\)0.15203025

Step 2. Bayes criterion (Expected Value) using \(EV(A_i)=\sum p_j\cdot x_{ij}\).

\[ \begin{aligned} EV(A_1) &= 0.18(120)+0.17(95)+0.16(80)+0.15(60)+0.19(40)+0.15(20)\\ &=21.60+16.15+12.80+9.00+7.60+3.00=70.15,\\ EV(A_2) &= 0.18(110)+0.17(100)+0.16(85)+0.15(70)+0.19(55)+0.15(30)\\ &=19.80+17.00+13.60+10.50+10.45+4.50=75.85,\\ EV(A_3) &= 0.18(140)+0.17(90)+0.16(70)+0.15(65)+0.19(35)+0.15(25)\\ &=25.20+15.30+11.20+9.75+6.65+3.75=71.85. \end{aligned} \]

EV ranking (best \(\to\) worst):

\[ A_2\,(75.85) > A_3\,(71.85) > A_1\,(70.15). \]

Step 3. Maximin criterion.

\[ \begin{aligned} \min(A_1)&=\min\{120,95,80,60,40,20\}=20,\\ \min(A_2)&=\min\{110,100,85,70,55,30\}=30,\\ \min(A_3)&=\min\{140,90,70,65,35,25\}=25. \end{aligned} \]

Maximin ranking (best \(\to\) worst):

\[ A_2\,(\min=30) > A_3\,(\min=25) > A_1\,(\min=20). \]

Step 4. Minimax Regret criterion.

Find the best payoff in each state:

\[ \begin{aligned} \max(S_1)&=140,\ \max(S_2)=100,\ \max(S_3)=85,\\ \max(S_4)&=70,\ \max(S_5)=55,\ \max(S_6)=30. \end{aligned} \]

Build regret table using \(r_{ij}=\max(S_j)-x_{ij}\).

Alternative\(S_1\)\(S_2\)\(S_3\)\(S_4\)\(S_5\)\(S_6\)Max regret
\(A_1\)\(140-120=20\)\(100-95=5\)\(85-80=5\)\(70-60=10\)\(55-40=15\)\(30-20=10\)20
\(A_2\)\(140-110=30\)\(100-100=0\)\(85-85=0\)\(70-70=0\)\(55-55=0\)\(30-30=0\)30
\(A_3\)\(140-140=0\)\(100-90=10\)\(85-70=15\)\(70-65=5\)\(55-35=20\)\(30-25=5\)20

Minimax Regret ranking (best \(\to\) worst, lower is better):

\[ A_1\,(20)=A_3\,(20) > A_2\,(30). \]

There is a tie between \(A_1\) and \(A_3\) under Minimax Regret.

Step 5. Conclusion.

Bayes and Maximin both select \(A_2\). Minimax Regret gives a tie between \(A_1\) and \(A_3\). The ordered lists show why strategies may disagree even on the same payoff table.

A transport company must choose one fleet plan among six alternatives: \(B_1,B_2,B_3,B_4,B_5,B_6\). There are three fuel-demand states with probabilities:

\[ T_1:0.50,\quad T_2:0.30,\quad T_3:0.20, \]

and \(0.50+0.30+0.20=1.00\). Payoffs are in thousand USD. Apply Bayes (EV), Maximin, and Minimax Regret.

State of natureProbability\(B_1\)\(B_2\)\(B_3\)\(B_4\)\(B_5\)\(B_6\)
\(T_1\)0.5095901108510092
\(T_2\)0.30707560806578
\(T_3\)0.20304010553520
↑

Structured solution

Step 1. Build the payoff table.

Payoff table

State of natureProbability\(B_1\)\(B_2\)\(B_3\)\(B_4\)\(B_5\)\(B_6\)
\(T_1\)0.5095901108510092
\(T_2\)0.30707560806578
\(T_3\)0.20304010553520

Step 2. Bayes criterion (Expected Value).

\[ \begin{aligned} EV(B_1)&=0.50(95)+0.30(70)+0.20(30)=74.50,\\ EV(B_2)&=0.50(90)+0.30(75)+0.20(40)=75.50,\\ EV(B_3)&=0.50(110)+0.30(60)+0.20(10)=75.00,\\ EV(B_4)&=0.50(85)+0.30(80)+0.20(55)=77.50,\\ EV(B_5)&=0.50(100)+0.30(65)+0.20(35)=76.50,\\ EV(B_6)&=0.50(92)+0.30(78)+0.20(20)=73.40. \end{aligned} \]

EV ranking (best \(\to\) worst):

\[ B_4\,(77.50)>B_5\,(76.50)>B_2\,(75.50)>B_3\,(75.00)>B_1\,(74.50)>B_6\,(73.40). \]

Step 3. Maximin criterion.

\[ \begin{aligned} \min(B_1)&=30,\ \min(B_2)=40,\ \min(B_3)=10,\\ \min(B_4)&=55,\ \min(B_5)=35,\ \min(B_6)=20. \end{aligned} \]

Maximin ranking (best \(\to\) worst):

\[ B_4\,(55)>B_2\,(40)>B_5\,(35)>B_1\,(30)>B_6\,(20)>B_3\,(10). \]

Step 4. Minimax Regret criterion.

Best payoff in each state:

\[ \max(T_1)=110,\quad \max(T_2)=80,\quad \max(T_3)=55. \]

Regret table:

Alternative\(T_1\)\(T_2\)\(T_3\)Max regret
\(B_1\)\(110-95=15\)\(80-70=10\)\(55-30=25\)25
\(B_2\)\(110-90=20\)\(80-75=5\)\(55-40=15\)20
\(B_3\)\(110-110=0\)\(80-60=20\)\(55-10=45\)45
\(B_4\)\(110-85=25\)\(80-80=0\)\(55-55=0\)25
\(B_5\)\(110-100=10\)\(80-65=15\)\(55-35=20\)20
\(B_6\)\(110-92=18\)\(80-78=2\)\(55-20=35\)35

Minimax Regret ranking (best \(\to\) worst, lower is better):

\[ B_2\,(20)=B_5\,(20)>B_1\,(25)=B_4\,(25)>B_6\,(35)>B_3\,(45). \]

Ties are explicit between \((B_2,B_5)\) and between \((B_1,B_4)\).

Step 5. Conclusion.

The three criteria lead to different priority orders, so decision choice depends on risk attitude.
