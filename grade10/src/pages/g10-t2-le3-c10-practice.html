A food-processing company must choose one packaging strategy for next quarter: \(P\) (premium line), \(Q\) (standard line), or \(R\) (hybrid line). Demand can be high (\(0.60\)) or low (\(0.40\)). Operating cost can be low (\(0.50\)), medium (\(0.30\)), or high (\(0.20\)).

Revenues (thousand USD):

  • High demand: \(P=500,\ Q=540,\ R=520\).
  • Low demand: \(P=320,\ Q=300,\ R=310\).

Costs (thousand USD):

  • \(P\): low \(180\), medium \(220\), high \(260\).
  • \(Q\): low \(210\), medium \(250\), high \(295\).
  • \(R\): low \(195\), medium \(235\), high \(275\).

Build the payoff table, apply EV, Maximin, and Minimax Regret, produce ordered rankings, then use a staged decision tree to choose a final strategy.

↑

Structured solution

Step 1. Build the payoff table from narrative data.

Joint state probabilities:

\[ \begin{aligned} &\Pr(H,L)=0.60\cdot 0.50=0.30,\quad \Pr(H,M)=0.60\cdot 0.30=0.18,\quad \Pr(H,Hc)=0.60\cdot 0.20=0.12,\\ &\Pr(L,L)=0.40\cdot 0.50=0.20,\quad \Pr(L,M)=0.40\cdot 0.30=0.12,\quad \Pr(L,Hc)=0.40\cdot 0.20=0.08. \end{aligned} \]

Payoff = revenue \(-\) cost.

State of natureProbability\(P\)\(Q\)\(R\)
High demand, low cost0.30\(500-180=320\)\(540-210=330\)\(520-195=325\)
High demand, medium cost0.18\(500-220=280\)\(540-250=290\)\(520-235=285\)
High demand, high cost0.12\(500-260=240\)\(540-295=245\)\(520-275=245\)
Low demand, low cost0.20\(320-180=140\)\(300-210=90\)\(310-195=115\)
Low demand, medium cost0.12\(320-220=100\)\(300-250=50\)\(310-235=75\)
Low demand, high cost0.08\(320-260=60\)\(300-295=5\)\(310-275=35\)

Step 2. Bayes criterion (EV).

\[ \begin{aligned} EV(P)&=0.30(320)+0.18(280)+0.12(240)+0.20(140)+0.12(100)+0.08(60)\\ &=96+50.4+28.8+28+12+4.8=220.0,\\ EV(Q)&=0.30(330)+0.18(290)+0.12(245)+0.20(90)+0.12(50)+0.08(5)\\ &=99+52.2+29.4+18+6+0.4=205.0,\\ EV(R)&=0.30(325)+0.18(285)+0.12(245)+0.20(115)+0.12(75)+0.08(35)\\ &=97.5+51.3+29.4+23+9+2.8=213.0. \end{aligned} \]

EV ranking:

\[ P\,(220.0)>R\,(213.0)>Q\,(205.0). \]

Step 3. Maximin criterion.

\[ \min(P)=60,\quad \min(Q)=5,\quad \min(R)=35. \]

Maximin ranking:

\[ P\,(60)>R\,(35)>Q\,(5). \]

Step 4. Minimax Regret criterion.

State best payoffs:

\[ \max=\{330,290,245,140,100,60\}. \]

Regret table:

Alternative\(HL\)\(HM\)\(HHc\)\(LL\)\(LM\)\(LHc\)Max regret
\(P\)\(330-320=10\)\(290-280=10\)\(245-240=5\)\(140-140=0\)\(100-100=0\)\(60-60=0\)10
\(Q\)\(330-330=0\)\(290-290=0\)\(245-245=0\)\(140-90=50\)\(100-50=50\)\(60-5=55\)55
\(R\)\(330-325=5\)\(290-285=5\)\(245-245=0\)\(140-115=25\)\(100-75=25\)\(60-35=25\)25

Minimax Regret ranking (lower is better):

\[ P\,(10)>R\,(25)>Q\,(55). \]

Step 5. Staged decision tree and final choice.

Filtering rule: keep best 2 EV options, then choose smaller Minimax Regret.

Decision tree
\(D_0\): \(\{P,Q,R\}\)
\(\rightarrow D_1\) (EV filter): keep \(\{P,R\}\)
\(\rightarrow D_2\) (Minimax Regret): compare \(P(10)\) vs \(R(25)\)
Leaves: \(P\), \(R\)
Final leaf selected: \(P\)

Final explanation: \(P\) is first in EV and Maximin, and also has the smallest maximum regret, so the tree process consistently ends at \(P\).

An online retailer must choose one pricing strategy among six options \(S_1,S_2,S_3,S_4,S_5,S_6\). Demand states are optimistic (\(0.45\)), regular (\(0.35\)), and weak (\(0.20\)). Payoffs (thousand USD):

StateProbability\(S_1\)\(S_2\)\(S_3\)\(S_4\)\(S_5\)\(S_6\)
Optimistic0.45150140170130160145
Regular0.35901008011095105
Weak0.202045-10603525

Apply EV, Maximin, and Minimax Regret step by step, generate full ordered rankings, and then apply this staged rule: keep best 4 EV options \(\rightarrow\) keep best 3 Maximin options \(\rightarrow\) choose smallest Minimax Regret.

↑

Structured solution

Step 1. EV calculations.

\[ \begin{aligned} EV(S_1)&=0.45(150)+0.35(90)+0.20(20)=103.00,\\ EV(S_2)&=0.45(140)+0.35(100)+0.20(45)=107.00,\\ EV(S_3)&=0.45(170)+0.35(80)+0.20(-10)=102.50,\\ EV(S_4)&=0.45(130)+0.35(110)+0.20(60)=109.00,\\ EV(S_5)&=0.45(160)+0.35(95)+0.20(35)=112.25,\\ EV(S_6)&=0.45(145)+0.35(105)+0.20(25)=107.00. \end{aligned} \]

EV ranking:

\[ S_5\,(112.25)>S_4\,(109.00)>S_2\,(107.00)=S_6\,(107.00)>S_1\,(103.00)>S_3\,(102.50). \]

There is a tie between \(S_2\) and \(S_6\).

Step 2. Maximin calculations.

\[ \begin{aligned} \min(S_1)&=20,\ \min(S_2)=45,\ \min(S_3)=-10,\\ \min(S_4)&=60,\ \min(S_5)=35,\ \min(S_6)=25. \end{aligned} \]

Maximin ranking:

\[ S_4\,(60)>S_2\,(45)>S_5\,(35)>S_6\,(25)>S_1\,(20)>S_3\,(-10). \]

Step 3. Minimax Regret calculations.

Best state payoffs:

\[ \max(\text{Optimistic})=170,\quad \max(\text{Regular})=110,\quad \max(\text{Weak})=60. \]

Regret table:

AlternativeOptimisticRegularWeakMax regret
\(S_1\)\(170-150=20\)\(110-90=20\)\(60-20=40\)40
\(S_2\)\(170-140=30\)\(110-100=10\)\(60-45=15\)30
\(S_3\)\(170-170=0\)\(110-80=30\)\(60-(-10)=70\)70
\(S_4\)\(170-130=40\)\(110-110=0\)\(60-60=0\)40
\(S_5\)\(170-160=10\)\(110-95=15\)\(60-35=25\)25
\(S_6\)\(170-145=25\)\(110-105=5\)\(60-25=35\)35

Minimax Regret ranking (lower is better):

\[ S_5\,(25)>S_2\,(30)>S_6\,(35)>S_1\,(40)=S_4\,(40)>S_3\,(70). \]

There is a tie between \(S_1\) and \(S_4\).

Step 4. Decision tree for the staged rule.

  • EV top 4: \(\{S_5,S_4,S_2,S_6\}\).
  • On this subset, Maximin top 3: \(S_4(60),S_2(45),S_5(35)\), so keep \(\{S_4,S_2,S_5\}\).
  • On this subset, Minimax Regret values are \(S_5(25),S_2(30),S_4(40)\).
Decision tree
\(D_0\): \(\{S_1,S_2,S_3,S_4,S_5,S_6\}\)
\(\rightarrow D_1\) (EV filter): \(\{S_5,S_4,S_2,S_6\}\)
\(\rightarrow D_2\) (Maximin filter): \(\{S_4,S_2,S_5\}\)
\(\rightarrow D_3\) (Minimax Regret choice): compare \(S_4(40),S_2(30),S_5(25)\)
Leaves: \(S_4\), \(S_2\), \(S_5\)
Final leaf selected: \(S_5\)

Step 5. Final conclusion.

The final decision is \(S_5\) because it is first in EV, third but acceptable in Maximin after filtering, and best in Minimax Regret among finalists.
