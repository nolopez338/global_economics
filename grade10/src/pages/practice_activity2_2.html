Narrative problem description

A firm is considering launching a project whose profit depends on whether state \(S_1\) (probability \(p\)) or state \(S_2\) (probability \(1-p\)) occurs. What is the decision maker trying to achieve by choosing the project, what constraints exist in committing to the launch, what real-world uncertainty drives the states of nature, why is the project the relevant alternative, and why do payoffs differ across the two states?


Question: Use expected value to determine the values of \(p\) for which the project is profitable.

Summary description

  • Economic context and transaction: a firm is evaluating whether to launch a single project based on expected profit.
  • Decision alternatives: undertake the project (single alternative evaluated with expected value).
  • States of nature: \(S_1\) and \(S_2\).
  • State probabilities: \(P(S_1)=p\), \(P(S_2)=1-p\).
  • Payoffs defined as: project profit is 18 in \(S_1\) and -4 in \(S_2\), as shown in the table.

Payoff table:
S1
\((p)\)		S2
\((1-p)\)
Project18-4
↑

Structured solution

\[ EV = 18p + (-4)(1-p) = 22p - 4 \] Profitability condition: \[ EV>0 \Rightarrow 22p-4>0 \]

Narrative problem description

An investor is choosing between two investment options whose returns depend on whether state \(S_1\) or \(S_2\) occurs. What is the decision maker trying to achieve, what constraints exist in selecting a single option, what uncertainty drives the states of nature, why are options A and B the realistic choices, and why do payoffs differ across the states?


Question: Let \(p\) be the probability of state \(S_1\). When is option A better than option B using Expected Value?

Summary description

  • Economic context and transaction: comparing investment returns under uncertainty.
  • Decision alternatives: option A; option B.
  • States of nature: \(S_1\) and \(S_2\).
  • State probabilities: \(P(S_1)=p\), \(P(S_2)=1-p\).
  • Payoffs defined as: A pays 22 in \(S_1\) and 6 in \(S_2\); B pays 16 in \(S_1\) and 10 in \(S_2\).

Payoff table:
S1
\((p)\)		S2
\((1-p)\)
A226
B1610
↑

Structured solution

\[ EV(A)=22p+6(1-p)=16p+6 \] \[ EV(B)=16p+10(1-p)=6p+10 \] Decision condition: \[ EV(A)-EV(B)>0 \] \[ (16p+6)-(6p+10)>0 \Rightarrow 10p-4>0 \]

Narrative problem description

A firm must choose among three production plans whose profits depend on whether state \(S_1\) or \(S_2\) occurs. What is the decision maker trying to achieve, what constraints exist in committing to one plan, what uncertainty drives the states of nature, why are plans A, B, and C the realistic options, and why do payoffs differ across the states?


Question: Let \(p\) be the probability of state \(S_1\). Determine for which values of \(p\) each plan is optimal.

Summary description

  • Economic context and transaction: selecting a production plan under uncertain outcomes.
  • Decision alternatives: plan A; plan B; plan C.
  • States of nature: \(S_1\) and \(S_2\).
  • State probabilities: \(P(S_1)=p\), \(P(S_2)=1-p\).
  • Payoffs defined as: A pays 30 in \(S_1\) and 0 in \(S_2\); B pays 20 in \(S_1\) and 8 in \(S_2\); C pays 14 in \(S_1\) and 12 in \(S_2\).

Payoff table:
S1
\((p)\)		S2
\((1-p)\)
A300
B208
C1412
↑

Structured solution

\[ EV(A)=30p,\quad EV(B)=20p+8(1-p)=12p+8,\quad EV(C)=14p+12(1-p)=2p+12 \] Pairwise comparison conditions in \(>0\) form: \[ EV(A)-EV(B)>0 \Rightarrow 30p-(12p+8)>0 \Rightarrow 18p-8>0 \] \[ EV(B)-EV(C)>0 \Rightarrow (12p+8)-(2p+12)>0 \Rightarrow 10p-4>0 \] \[ EV(A)-EV(C)>0 \Rightarrow 30p-(2p+12)>0 \Rightarrow 28p-12>0 \]

  • Payoffs defined as: A pays 20 in \(S_1\), 10 in \(S_2\), -5 in \(S_3\); B pays 12 in \(S_1\), 14 in \(S_2\), 0 in \(S_3\).

Payoff table:
S1
\((p_1)\)		S2
\((p_2)\)		S3
\((1-p_1-p_2)\)
