Problem 1: Left-tail inverse normal (positive and negative z)

Exam scores follow \(X\sim N(68,8^2)\).

C7

\[P(X<x_1)=0.8023,\qquad P(X<x_2)=0.1151.\] \[\Phi(z)=0.8023\Rightarrow z_1=0.85,\quad \Phi(z)=0.1151\Rightarrow z_2=-1.20.\] \[Z=\frac{X-68}{8},\qquad x=68+z(8).\] \[x_1=74.8,\qquad x_2=58.4.\]

Interpretation: About 80.23\% score below 74.8; 11.51\% score below 58.4.

Problem 2: Right-tail inverse normal (positive and negative z)

Delivery times follow \(X\sim N(40,6^2)\).

C7

\[P(X>x_1)=0.0885,\qquad P(X>x_2)=0.7580.\] \[P(X\le x_1)=0.9115\Rightarrow z_1=\Phi^{-1}(0.9115)=1.35.\] \[P(X\le x_2)=0.2420\Rightarrow z_2=\Phi^{-1}(0.2420)=-0.70.\] \[Z=\frac{X-40}{6},\quad x=40+z(6).\] \[x_1=48.1,\qquad x_2=35.8.\]

Interpretation: 8.85\% exceed 48.1 min; 75.80\% exceed 35.8 min.

Problem 3: Mixed left-tail and right-tail cutoff

Monthly sales (\$000) follow \(X\sim N(52,10^2)\).

C7

\[P(X<x_1)=0.7088,\qquad P(X>x_2)=0.1841.\] \[z_1=\Phi^{-1}(0.7088)=0.55.\] \[P(X\le x_2)=0.8159\Rightarrow z_2=\Phi^{-1}(0.8159)=0.90.\] \[Z=\frac{X-52}{10},\quad x=52+z(10).\] \[x_1=57.5,\qquad x_2=61.0.\]

Interpretation: 70.88\% of months are below \$57.5k; 18.41\% exceed \$61.0k.

Problem 7: Two disjoint regions with positive z-bounds

Household electricity use follows \(X\sim N(420,60^2)\).

C7

\[P(X<x_1)=0.7088,\qquad P(X>x_2)=0.0495.\] \[z_1=0.55,\qquad P(X\le x_2)=0.9505\Rightarrow z_2=1.65.\] \[Z=\frac{X-420}{60},\quad x=420+z(60).\] \[x_1=453.0,\qquad x_2=519.0.\]

Interpretation: 70.88\% are below 453 kWh; 4.95\% are above 519 kWh.

Problem 9: Two regions with both negative z-bounds

Commute times follow \(X\sim N(50,8^2)\).

C7

\[P(X<x_1)=0.0287,\qquad P(X>x_2)=0.6368.\] \[z_1=-1.90,\qquad P(X\le x_2)=0.3632\Rightarrow z_2=-0.35.\] \[Z=\frac{X-50}{8},\quad x=50+z(8).\] \[x_1=34.8,\qquad x_2=47.2.\]

Interpretation: 2.87\% commute under 34.8 min; 63.68\% exceed 47.2 min.
