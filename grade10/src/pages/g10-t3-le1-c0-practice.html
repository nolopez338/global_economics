Let \(X\) be the number of app notifications a student receives in one hour. The probability mass function is

\(x\)0123
\(P(X=x)\)0.20.3\(k\)0.1

Question.

  • (a) Determine \(k\) so that this is a valid probability distribution.
  • (b) State the nonnegativity restriction on \(k\).
  • (c) Using your value of \(k\), compute \(P(X\le 2)\).
  • (d) Interpret this probability in context.

Structured solution

For a valid pmf,

\[0.2+0.3+k+0.1=1\Rightarrow k=0.4.\]

Nonnegativity requires \(k\ge 0\), and \(k=0.4\) satisfies this.

\[P(X\le 2)=P(X=0)+P(X=1)+P(X=2)=0.2+0.3+0.4=0.9.\]

Interpretation. There is a \(0.9\) probability the student gets at most 2 notifications in an hour.

A quality check records \(X\), the number of defective items in a small batch.

\(x\)0123
\(P(X=x)\)0.5\(k\)0.20.1

Question. (a) Determine \(k\). (b) State nonnegativity restriction on \(k\). (c) Compute \(P(1\le X\le 2)\). (d) Interpret in context.

\[0.5+k+0.2+0.1=1\Rightarrow k=0.2.\]

Nonnegativity requires \(k\ge0\), and \(k=0.2\) is valid.

\[P(1\le X\le2)=P(X=1)+P(X=2)=0.2+0.2=0.4.\]

Interpretation. The probability of getting 1 or 2 defective items is \(0.4\).

\[\begin{array}{c|cccc}x&0&1&2&3\\\hline P(X=x)&0.1&0.4&p&2p\end{array}\]

Question. (a) Determine \(p\). (b) Nonnegativity. (c) Compute \(P(X\ge 2)\). (d) Interpret.

Solution. \[0.1+0.4+p+2p=1\Rightarrow p=\frac{1}{6}.\] Nonnegativity gives \(p\ge0\). \[P(X\ge2)=p+2p=\frac{1}{2}.\] Interpretation. Chance of at least 2 late deliveries is \(0.5\).

\[\begin{array}{c|cccc}x&1&2&3&4\\\hline P(X=x)&a&2a&3a&0.1\end{array}\]

Question. (a) Find \(a\). (b) Nonnegativity. (c) \(P(X\ne 4)\). (d) Interpret.

Solution. \[a+2a+3a+0.1=1\Rightarrow a=0.15.\] Nonnegativity gives \(a\ge0\). \[P(X\ne4)=1-0.1=0.9.\] Interpretation. Probability customer count differs from 4 is \(0.9\).

\[P(X=x)=\begin{cases}k,&x=0,\\2k,&x=1,\\3k,&x=2,\\2k,&x=3,\\1-8k,&x=4.\end{cases}\]

Question. (a) Determine all valid \(k\). (b) State nonnegativity restrictions. (c) Compute \(P(X\ge3)\) in terms of \(k\). (d) Interpret.

Solution. Sum condition is true for all \(k\). Nonnegativity gives \(0\le k\le\frac18\). \[P(X\ge3)=2k+(1-8k)=1-6k.\] Interpretation. Probability of at least 3 correct answers is \(1-6k\), valid for \(0\le k\le\frac18\).

\[\begin{array}{c|ccccc}x&0&1&2&3&4\\\hline P(M=x)&k&2k&3k&2k&k\end{array}\]

Solution. \[9k=1\Rightarrow k=\frac19,\quad P(M\ge3)=2k+k=\frac13.\] Nonnegativity: \(k\ge0\). Interpretation: chance of at least 3 messages is \(\frac13\).

\[P(G=x)=\begin{cases}p,&x=0,\\\frac34-2p,&x=1,\\p,&x=2,\\\frac14,&x=3.\end{cases}\]

Valid \(p\): \(0\le p\le\frac38\). \[P(G\in\{0,2,3\})=2p+\frac14.\] Interpretation in context as in source.

\[\begin{array}{c|cccc}x&0&1&2&3\\\hline P(B=x)&a&b&2a&b\end{array}\],\quad P(B=0)=P(B=3).\]

From \(a=b\) and \(a+b+2a+b=1\), \(a=b=\frac15\). \[P(B>1)=2a+b=\frac35.\] Interpretation: probability of more than one late bus is \(\frac35\).

\[\begin{array}{c|ccccc}x&0&1&2&3&4\\\hline P(C=x)&k&2k&4k&2k&k\end{array}\]

\(10k=1\Rightarrow k=\frac{1}{10}\), \(k\ge0\). \[P(1\le C\le3)=2k+4k+2k=\frac45.\] Interpretation: chance of 1 to 3 customers is \(\frac45\).

\[\begin{array}{c|cccc}x&0&1&2&3\\\hline P(Q=x)&2p&p&\frac12-p&\frac14\end{array}\]

\(p=\frac18\), with nonnegativity \(0\le p\le \frac12\). \[P(Q\in\{0,2\})=2p+\left(\frac12-p\right)=\frac58.\]

\[\begin{array}{c|ccccc}x&0&1&2&3&4\\\hline P(Y=x)&a&b&a+b&2a&2b\end{array}\],\quad P(Y=4)=2P(Y=0).\]

From \(a+b=\frac14\) and \(b=a\), \(a=b=\frac18\). \[P(Y\le1)=a+b=\frac14.\]

\[P(D=x)=\begin{cases}p,&x=0,\\2p,&x=1,\\\frac12-2p,&x=2,\\p,&x=3,\\\frac14,&x=4.\end{cases}\]

\(p=\frac18\), nonnegativity \(0\le p\le\frac14\). \[P(D\in\{0,2,4\})=p+\left(\frac12-2p\right)+\frac14=\frac58.\]

\[\begin{array}{c|cccc}x&0&1&2&3\\\hline P(N=x)&\frac{k}{2}&k&\frac{3k}{2}&k\end{array}\]

\(4k=1\Rightarrow k=\frac14\), \(k\ge0\). \[P(N\le1)=\frac{k}{2}+k=\frac38.\]

\[\begin{array}{c|ccccc}x&0&1&2&3&4\\\hline P(H=x)&p&2p&3p&\frac12-2p&\frac14\end{array}\]

\(4p+\frac34=1\Rightarrow p=\frac1{16}\), with \(0\le p\le\frac14\). \[P(H>2)=\left(\frac12-2p\right)+\frac14=\frac58.\]

\[\begin{array}{c|ccccc}x&0&1&2&3&4\\\hline P(R=x)&k&2k&2k&k&k\end{array}\]

\(7k=1\Rightarrow k=\frac17\), \(k\ge0\). \[P(R\ne2)=1-2k=\frac57.\] Interpretation. There is a \(\frac57\) chance the counter processes a number of returns different from 2.
