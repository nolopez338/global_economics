Problem. Let \(X\) be the number of app notifications a student receives in one hour. The probability mass function is

\[\begin{array}{c|cccc}x & 0 & 1 & 2 & 3\\\hline P(X=x) & 0.2 & 0.3 & k & 0.1\end{array}\]

Question.

  • (a) Determine \(k\) so that this is a valid probability distribution.
  • (b) Compute: (i) \(P(X=2)\), (ii) \(P(X\le 2)\), and (iii) \(P(1\le X\le 2)\).
  • (c) Interpret these results in terms of percentage probability.

Problem. Let \(T\) be the number of goals scored by a team in a match summary. The probability mass function is

\[P(T=t)=\begin{cases}p, & t=0,\\\frac{3}{4}-2p, & t=1,\\p, & t=2,\\\frac{1}{4}, & t=3.\end{cases}\]

Question.

  • (a) Determine all valid values of \(p\). Since all listed probabilities must satisfy nonnegativity, require \(p\ge 0,\qquad \frac{3}{4}-2p\ge 0,\) so the feasible interval is \(0\le p\le \frac{3}{8}.\)
  • (b) Compute: (i) \(P(T=1)\), (ii) \(P(T\in\{0,2,3\})\), and (iii) \(P(1\le T\le 3)\).
  • (c) Interpret these results in terms of percentage probability.

  • (a) Determine \(u\) and \(v\) so that the model is valid.
  • (b) Compute: (i) \(P(X=2)\), (ii) \(P(X>1)\), and (iii) \(P(1\le X\le 3)\).
  • (c) Interpret these results in terms of percentage probability.
