Demand and cost conditions are independent.

A logistics firm is choosing a distribution plan for the next three years and wants a strategy that can handle uncertain shipment demand and volatile operating costs. Management is considering three alternatives: building a single central hub, investing in multiple regional hubs, or relying on flexible partner networks.

The firm models demand as either high or low. If demand is high, the expected annual revenues (in million USD) are 120 for a central hub, 100 for regional hubs, and 90 for flexible partners, with a 0.30 probability. If demand is low, revenues are expected to be 70, 80, and 75 respectively, with a 0.70 probability. Independently, operating costs can be favourable or unfavourable. Under favourable costs (probability 0.40), annual costs are estimated at 50 for a central hub, 45 for regional hubs, and 40 for flexible partners. Under unfavourable costs (probability 0.60), annual costs are 80, 65, and 55 in the same order. The firm assumes demand conditions and cost conditions are independent.

Question: Make a decision on the best option based on three perspectives: safe behavior (Maximin), balanced behavior (Expected Value), and risky behavior (Maximax).

↑
Maximax selects the alternative with the highest best payoff. \[ \begin{aligned} \max_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Expanded}, S_i) &= \max\{18, 12, 3, -3\} = 18 \\ \max_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Classic}, S_i) &= \max\{14, 12, 10, 8\} = 14 \end{aligned} \] \[ \max\{18, 14\} = 18 \] The maximax (risk-seeking) decision is expand the menu.
↑
Maximin compares each alternative's worst payoff. \[ \begin{aligned} \min_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Expanded}, S_i) &= \min\{18, 12, 3, -3\} = -3 \\ \min_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Classic}, S_i) &= \min\{14, 12, 10, 8\} = 8 \end{aligned} \] \[ \max\{-3, 8\} = 8 \] The maximin (risk-averse) decision is keep the classic menu.
↑
The truck wants the menu that maximizes expected profit given the joint demand-cost probabilities. \[ \begin{aligned} EV_{\text{Expanded}} &= 0.30(18) + 0.20(12) + 0.30(3) + 0.20(-3) \\ &= 5.4 + 2.4 + 0.9 - 0.6 = 8.1 \\ EV_{\text{Classic}} &= 0.30(14) + 0.20(12) + 0.30(10) + 0.20(8) \\ &= 4.2 + 2.4 + 3 + 1.6 = 11.2 \end{aligned} \] \[ \max\{8.1, 11.2\} = 11.2 \] Summary of recommendations across decision criteria
  • Maximax favors expanded menu (best payoff of 18).
  • Maximin favors classic menu (best worst-case payoff of 8).
  • Expected value favors classic menu (EV of 11.2).
The expanded menu offers the highest upside, but the classic menu provides stronger protection and higher average profit.
