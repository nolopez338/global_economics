Problem. Let \(X\) be a continuous random variable with density

\[ f(x)=\frac{x}{18},\quad 0\le x\le 6, \]

and \(f(x)=0\) otherwise.

Question. (a) State the support of \(X\). (b) Verify geometrically that \(f\) is a valid PDF. (c) Compute \(P(0\le X\le 3)\).

↑

Solution.

(a) The support is \([0,6]\).

(b) The graph is a single straight line from \((0,0)\) to \((6,\tfrac13)\). So total area is triangle area:

\[ \frac12(6)\left(\frac13\right)=1. \]

Hence \(f\) is valid.

(c) On \([0,3]\), the area is a smaller triangle with height \(f(3)=\frac16\):

\[ P(0\le X\le 3)=\frac12(3)\left(\frac16\right)=\frac14. \]

Graph with support \([0,6]\) and shaded probability region \([0,3]\).

Interpretation. The model increases linearly, so larger values in the support are relatively more likely than very small values.

Problem. A random variable \(Y\) has density

\[ f(y)=\begin{cases} \dfrac{y}{16}, & 0\le y\le 4,\\ \dfrac{8-y}{16}, & 4\le y\le 8,\\ 0, & \text{otherwise.} \end{cases} \]

Question. (a) Verify this is a valid PDF. (b) Compute \(P(2\le Y\le 6)\). (c) Identify the most likely region and explain why.

↑

Solution.

(a) The graph is a triangle with base \(8\) and peak height \(f(4)=\frac14\).

\[ \text{Area}=\frac12(8)\left(\frac14\right)=1. \]

So it is valid.

(b) Use complement:

\[ P(2\le Y\le 6)=1-P(0\le Y\le 2)-P(6\le Y\le 8). \]

Each side area is a triangle with base \(2\) and height \(\frac18\):

\[ \frac12(2)\left(\frac18\right)=\frac18. \]

Hence

\[ P(2\le Y\le 6)=1-\frac18-\frac18=\frac34. \]

Graph with symmetric triangular density and shaded interval \([2,6]\).

Interpretation. Values near \(y=4\) are most likely because the density reaches its highest point there.

Problem. A random variable \(Z\) has density

\[ f(z)=\begin{cases} \dfrac{z}{20}, & 0\le z\le 4,\\ \dfrac{10-z}{30}, & 4\le z\le 10,\\ 0, & \text{otherwise.} \end{cases} \]

Question. (a) Verify the model is a valid PDF. (b) Compute \(P(1\le Z\le 7)\). (c) Where is \(Z\) most likely to be found?

↑

Solution.

(a) Peak at \(z=4\) has height \(\frac15\). Left triangle area: \(\frac12(4)(\frac15)=\frac25\). Right triangle area: \(\frac12(6)(\frac15)=\frac35\). Total \(=1\).

(b) Compute by complement:

\[ P(1\le Z\le 7)=1-P(0\le Z\le 1)-P(7\le Z\le 10). \] \[ P(0\le Z\le 1)=\frac12(1)\left(\frac1{20}\right)=\frac1{40}. \]

At \(z=7\), \(f(7)=\frac{1}{10}\), so

\[ P(7\le Z\le 10)=\frac12(3)\left(\frac{1}{10}\right)=\frac{3}{20}. \]

Therefore

\[ P(1\le Z\le 7)=1-\frac1{40}-\frac3{20}=\frac{33}{40}=0.825. \]

Graph with asymmetric triangular density and shaded interval \([1,7]\).

Interpretation. The peak at \(z=4\) marks the most likely region, but the longer right side spreads mass across larger values.

Problem. Let \(T\) have density

\[ f(t)=\begin{cases} \dfrac{t}{9}, & 0\le t\le 3,\\ \dfrac{6-t}{9}, & 3\le t\le 6,\\ 0, & \text{otherwise.} \end{cases} \]

Question. (a) Verify validity. (b) Compute \(P(2\le T\le 5)\) by splitting at the peak. (c) Compare \(P(1\le T\le 2)\) and \(P(4\le T\le 5)\).

↑

Solution.

(a) Triangle base \(6\), height \(\frac13\) gives area

\[ \frac12(6)\left(\frac13\right)=1. \]

(b) Split \([2,5]\) into \([2,3]\) and \([3,5]\).

\[ A_{[2,3]}=\frac{\left(\frac29+\frac13\right)}{2}\cdot1=\frac{5}{18}, \quad A_{[3,5]}=\frac{\left(\frac13+\frac19\right)}{2}\cdot2=\frac{4}{9}. \]

So

\[ P(2\le T\le 5)=\frac{5}{18}+\frac{4}{9}=\frac{13}{18}. \]

(c) By symmetry around \(t=3\), these two one-unit intervals have equal probability.

Graph with triangular density and shaded interval \([2,5]\).

Interpretation. The central region around \(t=3\) is most probable because the graph is highest there.

Problem. Let \(W\) have density

\[ f(w)=\begin{cases} \dfrac{w}{20}, & 0\le w\le 5,\\ \dfrac{8-w}{12}, & 5\le w\le 8,\\ 0, & \text{otherwise.} \end{cases} \]

Question. (a) Verify this is a valid PDF. (b) Compute \(P(3\le W\le 7)\) by splitting at \(w=5\). (c) Which is larger: \(P(2\le W\le 4)\) or \(P(6\le W\le 8)\)? Explain from the graph.

↑

Solution.

(a) Peak height is \(f(5)=\frac14\).

\[ \text{Area}=\frac12(5)\left(\frac14\right)+\frac12(3)\left(\frac14\right)=\frac58+\frac38=1. \]

(b)

\[ A_{[3,5]}=\frac{\left(\frac3{20}+\frac14\right)}{2}\cdot2=\frac25, \quad A_{[5,7]}=\frac{\left(\frac14+\frac1{12}\right)}{2}\cdot2=\frac13. \]

Hence

\[ P(3\le W\le 7)=\frac25+\frac13=\frac{11}{15}. \]

(c)

\[ P(2\le W\le 4)=\frac{\left(\frac1{10}+\frac15\right)}{2}\cdot2=\frac3{10}, \quad P(6\le W\le 8)=\frac12(2)\left(\frac16\right)=\frac16. \]

So \(P(2\le W\le 4)>P(6\le W\le 8)\).

Graph with piecewise linear density and shaded interval \([3,7]\).

Interpretation. Because the peak is closer to 5 and the left side is wider, mid-left intervals can hold more area than far-right intervals.
