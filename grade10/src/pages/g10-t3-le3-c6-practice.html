Problem. Exam scores follow \(X\sim N(70,8^2)\). Find \(P(X<82)\) and \(P(X<60.4)\).

\[Z=\frac{X-70}{8}.\]
\[P(X<82)=P\!\left(Z<\frac{82-70}{8}\right)=P(Z<1.50)=0.9332.\]
\[P(X<60.4)=P\!\left(Z<\frac{60.4-70}{8}\right)=P(Z<-1.20)=1-0.8849=0.1151.\]

Interpretation. About 93.32% score below 82, while 11.51% score below 60.4.

Problem. Delivery times follow \(X\sim N(56,5^2)\) minutes. Find \(P(X>63.5)\) and \(P(X>52)\).

\[Z=\frac{X-56}{5}.\]
\[P(X>63.5)=P\!\left(Z>\frac{63.5-56}{5}\right)=P(Z>1.50)=1-0.9332=0.0668.\]
\[P(X>52)=P\!\left(Z>\frac{52-56}{5}\right)=P(Z>-0.80)=\Phi(0.80)=0.7881.\]

Interpretation. 6.68% take more than 63.5 min, and 78.81% take more than 52 min.

Problem. Production weights follow \(X\sim N(500,20^2)\) g. Find \(P(X<514)\) and \(P(X>483)\).

\[Z=\frac{X-500}{20}.\]
\[P(X<514)=P\!\left(Z<\frac{514-500}{20}\right)=P(Z<0.70)=0.7580.\]
\[P(X>483)=P\!\left(Z>\frac{483-500}{20}\right)=P(Z>-0.85)=\Phi(0.85)=0.8023.\]

Interpretation. 75.80% are below 514 g, and 80.23% are above 483 g.

Problem. Weekly app usage follows \(X\sim N(14,4^2)\) hours. Find \(P(15<X<18.6)\).

\[Z=\frac{X-14}{4},\quad P(15<X<18.6)=P(0.25<Z<1.15).\]
\[P=\Phi(1.15)-\Phi(0.25)=0.8749-0.5987=0.2762.\]

Interpretation. 27.62% of students use the app between 15 h and 18.6 h weekly.

Problem. Commute times follow \(X\sim N(40,10^2)\) minutes. Find \(P(30.5<X<50.5)\).

\[Z=\frac{X-40}{10},\quad P(30.5<X<50.5)=P(-0.95<Z<1.05).\]
\[P=\Phi(1.05)-\Phi(-0.95)=0.8531-(1-0.8289)=0.6820.\]

Interpretation. 68.20% of commutes are between 30.5 and 50.5 minutes.

Problem. Monthly sales follow \(X\sim N(120,20^2)\) units. Find \(P(84<X<103)\).

\[Z=\frac{X-120}{20},\quad P(84<X<103)=P(-1.80<Z<-0.85).\]
\[P=\Phi(-0.85)-\Phi(-1.80)=(1-0.8023)-(1-0.9641)=0.1618.\]

Interpretation. 16.18% of months have sales between 84 and 103 units.

Problem. Household electricity use follows \(X\sim N(400,50^2)\) kWh. Find \(P(X<450\ \text{or}\ X>495)\).

\[Z=\frac{X-400}{50},\quad P(X<450\ \text{or}\ X>495)=P(Z<1.00\ \text{or}\ Z>1.90).\]

The regions are disjoint, so

\[P=\Phi(1.00)+\bigl(1-\Phi(1.90)\bigr)=0.8413+0.0287=0.8700.\]

Interpretation. 87.00% of homes are below 450 kWh or above 495 kWh.

Problem. Scholarship scores follow \(X\sim N(300,20^2)\). Find \(P(X<279\ \text{or}\ X>327)\).

\[Z=\frac{X-300}{20},\quad P(X<279\ \text{or}\ X>327)=P(Z<-1.05\ \text{or}\ Z>1.35).\]

The regions are disjoint, so

\[P=\Phi(-1.05)+\bigl(1-\Phi(1.35)\bigr)=(1-0.8531)+(1-0.9115)=0.2354.\]

Interpretation. 23.54% are either below 279 or above 327 points.

Problem. Customer waiting times follow \(X\sim N(90,20^2)\) seconds. Find \(P(X<66\ \text{or}\ X>73)\).

\[Z=\frac{X-90}{20},\quad P(X<66\ \text{or}\ X>73)=P(Z<-1.20\ \text{or}\ Z>-0.85).\]

The regions are disjoint, so

\[P=\Phi(-1.20)+\bigl(1-\Phi(-0.85)\bigr)=0.1151+0.8023=0.9174.\]

Interpretation. 91.74% wait below 66 s or above 73 s.
