Narrative problem description

A small café is deciding whether to launch a new line of artisan desserts or keep the current menu for daily sales, aiming to improve profit while customer foot traffic is uncertain. What is the owner trying to achieve, what constraints exist from committing to one menu strategy, what real-world uncertainty drives high versus low traffic, why are these two alternatives realistic, and why do payoffs differ across traffic levels?


Question: Make a decision on the best option based on three perspectives: safe behavior (Maximin), balanced behavior (Expected Value), and risky behavior (Maximax).

Summary description

  • Economic context and transaction: daily café sales with profit per dessert plate sold.
  • Decision alternatives: launch the dessert line; keep the current menu.
  • States of nature: high traffic; low traffic.
  • State probabilities: high traffic → 0.55, low traffic → 0.45.
  • Payoffs defined as: profit in thousand USD: launch dessert line → 42 (high), 8 (low); keep menu → 28 (high), 20 (low).

The maximax rule selects the alternative with the highest possible payoff. \[ \begin{aligned} \max_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Launch}, S_i) &= \max\{42, 8\} = 42 \\ \max_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Keep}, S_i) &= \max\{28, 20\} = 28 \end{aligned} \] \[ \max\{42, 28\} = 42 \] Because 42 is the largest best payoff, the maximax (risk-seeking) recommendation is launch the dessert line.
↑
The maximin rule focuses on the worst-case payoff of each alternative. \[ \begin{aligned} \min_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Launch}, S_i) &= \min\{42, 8\} = 8 \\ \min_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Keep}, S_i) &= \min\{28, 20\} = 20 \end{aligned} \] \[ \max\{8, 20\} = 20 \] Since 20 exceeds 8, the maximin (risk-averse) choice is keep the current menu.
↑
We choose the option that maximizes the café's profit objective. Alternatives are launch or keep the menu; states are high or low traffic. Expected values: \[ \begin{aligned} EV_{\text{Launch}} &= 0.55(42) + 0.45(8) = 23.1 + 3.6 = 26.7 \\ EV_{\text{Keep}} &= 0.55(28) + 0.45(20) = 15.4 + 9 = 24.4 \end{aligned} \] \[ \max\{26.7, 24.4\} = 26.7 \] Summary of recommendations across decision criteria
  • Maximax favors launching the dessert line (highest payoff of 42).
  • Maximin favors keeping the current menu (best worst-case payoff of 20).
  • Expected value favors launching the dessert line (EV of 26.7).
Launching desserts leads on optimistic and average outcomes, while the current menu protects the downside.

Narrative problem description

A renewable-energy company is selecting a generation mix for annual electricity sales and wants to maximize profit while weather conditions are uncertain. What is the decision maker trying to achieve, what constraints exist from committing capital to one mix, what real-world uncertainty drives windy, sunny, or cloudy states, why are wind, solar, and hybrid systems the realistic options, and why do payoffs differ across weather outcomes?


Question: Make a decision on the best strategy based on three perspectives: safe behavior (Maximin), balanced behavior (Expected Value), and risky behavior (Maximax).

Summary description

  • Economic context and transaction: annual electricity sales with profit per megawatt-hour generated and sold.
  • Decision alternatives: build wind turbines; build solar farms; build a balanced hybrid system.
  • States of nature: windy; sunny; cloudy.
  • State probabilities: windy → 0.30, sunny → 0.45, cloudy → 0.25.
  • Payoffs defined as: profit in thousand USD: wind turbines → 90 (windy), 45 (sunny), 30 (cloudy); solar farms → 35 (windy), 95 (sunny), 25 (cloudy); hybrid system → 70 (windy), 65 (sunny), 60 (cloudy).

  • Maximin favors the hybrid system (best worst-case payoff of 60).
  • Expected value favors the hybrid system (EV of 65.25).
Solar has the best upside, but the hybrid option balances downside protection with the strongest average profit.
