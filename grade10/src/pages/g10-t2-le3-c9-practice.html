A city logistics office must choose one policy among \(B_1,B_2,B_3,B_4,B_5,B_6\). Payoffs (thousand USD) and probabilities are:

StateProbability\(B_1\)\(B_2\)\(B_3\)\(B_4\)\(B_5\)\(B_6\)
\(T_1\)0.5095901108510092
\(T_2\)0.30707560806578
\(T_3\)0.20304010553520

Ordered criterion results (already computed):

\[ \text{EV ranking: }B_4(77.50)>B_5(76.50)>B_2(75.50)>B_3(75.00)>B_1(74.50)>B_6(73.40). \] \[ \text{Maximin ranking: }B_4(55)>B_2(40)>B_5(35)>B_1(30)>B_6(20)>B_3(10). \] \[ \text{Minimax Regret ranking: }B_2(20)=B_5(20)>B_1(25)=B_4(25)>B_6(35)>B_3(45). \]

Filtering rule: (1) keep the best 4 EV options; (2) from those, keep the best 2 Maximin options; (3) from those, choose the smallest Minimax Regret.

↑

Structured solution

Step 1. Reconfirm ordered lists.

  • EV top 4: \(\{B_4,B_5,B_2,B_3\}\).
  • On this subset, Maximin values are \(B_4(55),\ B_2(40),\ B_5(35),\ B_3(10)\), so top 2 are \(\{B_4,B_2\}\).
  • On \(\{B_4,B_2\}\), Max regrets are \(B_4(25),\ B_2(20)\).

Step 2. Decision tree for staged filtering.

Decision tree (filtering nodes and leaves)
Root decision node \(D_0\): All alternatives \(\{B_1,B_2,B_3,B_4,B_5,B_6\}\)
\(\rightarrow\) Node \(D_1\) (EV filter): keep \(\{B_4,B_5,B_2,B_3\}\)
\(\rightarrow\) Node \(D_2\) (Maximin filter): keep \(\{B_4,B_2\}\)
\(\rightarrow\) Node \(D_3\) (Minimax Regret filter): choose smallest max regret
Leaves: \(B_2\) (20), \(B_4\) (25)
Final leaf selected: \(B_2\)

Step 3. Final decision.

The decision tree selects \(B_2\).

A regional distributor must choose one expansion plan among \(X_1,X_2,X_3,X_4,X_5,X_6\). Payoffs (thousand USD) and probabilities are:

StateProbability\(X_1\)\(X_2\)\(X_3\)\(X_4\)\(X_5\)\(X_6\)
\(U_1\)0.30130120140110125118
\(U_2\)0.259095801008892
\(U_3\)0.25607055856575
\(U_4\)0.20204010604535

Ordered criterion results (already computed):

\[ \text{EV ranking: }X_4(91.25)>X_2(85.25)>X_5(84.75)>X_6(84.15)>X_1(80.50)>X_3(77.75). \] \[ \text{Maximin ranking: }X_4(60)>X_5(45)>X_2(40)>X_6(35)>X_1(20)>X_3(10). \] \[ \text{Minimax Regret ranking: }X_2(20)=X_5(20)>X_6(25)>X_4(30)>X_1(40)>X_3(50). \]

Filtering rule: (1) keep the best 3 EV options; (2) from those, keep the best 2 Minimax Regret options; (3) from those, choose the best Maximin option.

↑

Structured solution

Step 1. Reconfirm ordered lists and filtering sets.

  • EV top 3: \(\{X_4,X_2,X_5\}\).
  • In this subset, Minimax Regret values are \(X_2(20),X_5(20),X_4(30)\), so keep \(\{X_2,X_5\}\).
  • In \(\{X_2,X_5\}\), Maximin values are \(X_2(40),X_5(45)\).

Step 2. Decision tree for staged filtering.

Decision tree (filtering nodes and leaves)
Root decision node \(D_0\): \(\{X_1,X_2,X_3,X_4,X_5,X_6\}\)
\(\rightarrow\) Node \(D_1\) (EV filter): keep \(\{X_4,X_2,X_5\}\)
\(\rightarrow\) Node \(D_2\) (Minimax Regret filter): keep \(\{X_2,X_5\}\)
\(\rightarrow\) Node \(D_3\) (Maximin choice)
Leaves: \(X_2\) (min \(=40\)), \(X_5\) (min \(=45\))
Final leaf selected: \(X_5\)

Step 3. Final decision.

The decision tree selects \(X_5\).
