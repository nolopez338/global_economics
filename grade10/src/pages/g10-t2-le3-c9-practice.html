A city logistics office must choose one policy among \(B_1,B_2,B_3,B_4,B_5,B_6\). Payoffs (thousand USD) and probabilities are:

StateProbability\(B_1\)\(B_2\)\(B_3\)\(B_4\)\(B_5\)\(B_6\)
\(T_1\)0.5095901108510092
\(T_2\)0.30707560806578
\(T_3\)0.20304010553520

Ordered criterion results (already computed):

\[ \text{EV ranking: }B_4(77.50)>B_5(76.50)>B_2(75.50)>B_3(75.00)>B_1(74.50)>B_6(73.40). \] \[ \text{Maximin ranking: }B_4(55)>B_2(40)>B_5(35)>B_1(30)>B_6(20)>B_3(10). \] \[ \text{Minimax Regret ranking: }B_2(20)=B_5(20)>B_1(25)=B_4(25)>B_6(35)>B_3(45). \]

Filtering rule: (1) keep the best 4 EV options; (2) from those, keep the best 2 Maximin options; (3) from those, choose the smallest Minimax Regret.

↑

Structured solution

Step 1. Reconfirm ordered lists.

  • EV top 4: \(\{B_4,B_5,B_2,B_3\}\).
  • On this subset, Maximin values are \(B_4(55),\ B_2(40),\ B_5(35),\ B_3(10)\), so top 2 are \(\{B_4,B_2\}\).
  • On \(\{B_4,B_2\}\), Max regrets are \(B_4(25),\ B_2(20)\).

Step 2. Decision tree for staged filtering.

Decision tree (filtering nodes and leaves)
Root decision node \(D_0\): All alternatives \(\{B_1,B_2,B_3,B_4,B_5,B_6\}\)
\(\r\rightarrow\) Node \(D_1\) (EV filter): keep \(\{B_4,B_5,B_2,B_3\}\)
\(\r\rightarrow\) Node \(D_2\) (Maximin filter): keep \(\{B_4,B_2\}\)
\(\r\rightarrow\) Node \(D_3\) (Minimax Regret filter): choose smallest max regret
Leaves: \(B_2\) (20), \(B_4\) (25)
Final leaf selected: \(B_2\)

Step 3. Final decision.

The decision tree selects \(B_2\).
