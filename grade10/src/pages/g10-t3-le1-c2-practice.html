A learner waits for a bus that can arrive at any time between 0 and 12 minutes after reaching the stop. Let \(X\sim \text{Uniform}(0,12)\).

Question.

Find:

  • (a) \(P(X<5)\),
  • (b) \(P(3\le X\le 9)\).

Interpret each result in context.

↑

The total interval length is \[b-a=12-0=12.\]

For a uniform variable, \[P(\text{event})=\frac{\text{event interval length}}{\text{total length}}.\]

\[P(X<5)=\frac{5-0}{12}=\frac{5}{12}\approx 0.417.\]

\[P(3\le X\le 9)=\frac{9-3}{12}=\frac{6}{12}=\frac{1}{2}=0.5.\]

Interpretation. There is a \(\frac{5}{12}\) chance the wait is under 5 minutes, and a \(\frac{1}{2}\) chance the wait is between 3 and 9 minutes.

A file download takes a random time between 2 and 10 minutes. Let \(X\sim \text{Uniform}(2,10)\).

Question. Find: (a) \(P(4\le X\le 7)\), (b) \(P(X>8)\). Interpret each result.

The total interval length is \[b-a=10-2=8.\]

\[P(4\le X\le 7)=\frac{7-4}{8}=\frac{3}{8}=0.375.\]

\[P(X>8)=\frac{10-8}{8}=\frac{2}{8}=\frac{1}{4}=0.25.\]

Interpretation. The download is between 4 and 7 minutes with probability \(\frac{3}{8}\), and longer than 8 minutes with probability \(\frac{1}{4}\).

In a city, early-morning temperature (in \(^\circ\text{C}\)) is modeled as uniform from \(-3\) to \(5\). Let \(X\sim \text{Uniform}(-3,5)\).

Question. Find: (a) \(P(-1\le X\le 2)\), (b) \(P(X<0\ \text{or}\ X>4)\). Interpret each probability.

The total interval length is \[b-a=5-(-3)=8.\]

\[P(-1\le X\le 2)=\frac{2-(-1)}{8}=\frac{3}{8}=0.375.\]

For the union of disjoint intervals:

\[P(X<0\ \text{or}\ X>4)=\frac{0-(-3)}{8}+\frac{5-4}{8}=\frac{3}{8}+\frac{1}{8}=\frac{1}{2}=0.5.\]

Interpretation. There is a \(\frac{3}{8}\) chance the temperature is between \(-1^\circ\text{C}\) and \(2^\circ\text{C}\), and a \(\frac{1}{2}\) chance it is below \(0^\circ\text{C}\) or above \(4^\circ\text{C}\).

A delivery may arrive any time between minute 100 and minute 160 of a tracking window. Let \(X\sim \text{Uniform}(100,160)\).

Question. Find: (a) \(P(115\le X\le 145)\), (b) \(P(X<110\ \text{or}\ X>150)\). Interpret both answers.

The total interval length is \[b-a=160-100=60.\]

\[P(115\le X\le 145)=\frac{145-115}{60}=\frac{30}{60}=\frac{1}{2}=0.5.\]

\[P(X<110\ \text{or}\ X>150)=\frac{110-100}{60}+\frac{160-150}{60}=\frac{10}{60}+\frac{10}{60}=\frac{20}{60}=\frac{1}{3}.\]

Interpretation. The package has probability \(\frac{1}{2}\) of arriving between minutes 115 and 145, and probability \(\frac{1}{3}\) of arriving very early or very late in the window.

A student's after-school practice session can last between 30 and 90 minutes, uniformly. Let \(X\sim \text{Uniform}(30,90)\).

Question. Find: (a) \(P(X\le 50)\), (b) \(P(45\le X\le 75)\). Interpret each result.

The total interval length is \[b-a=90-30=60.\]

\[P(X\le 50)=\frac{50-30}{60}=\frac{20}{60}=\frac{1}{3}.\]

\[P(45\le X\le 75)=\frac{75-45}{60}=\frac{30}{60}=\frac{1}{2}=0.5.\]

Interpretation. There is a \(\frac{1}{3}\) chance practice ends by 50 minutes, and a \(\frac{1}{2}\) chance it lasts from 45 to 75 minutes.

A student's commute time (minutes) is uniformly distributed from 5 to 25. Let \(X\sim \text{Uniform}(5,25)\).

Question. Find: (a) \(P(10\le X\le 20)\), (b) \(P(X<10\ \text{or}\ X>20)\) using complement reasoning. Interpret each probability.

The total interval length is \[b-a=25-5=20.\]

\[P(10\le X\le 20)=\frac{20-10}{20}=\frac{10}{20}=\frac{1}{2}.\]

Using complement:

\[P(X<10\ \text{or}\ X>20)=1-P(10\le X\le 20)=1-\frac{1}{2}=\frac{1}{2}.\]

Interpretation. The commute is between 10 and 20 minutes half the time, and outside that range the other half.

An online game match lasts between 40 and 70 minutes, uniformly. Let \(X\sim \text{Uniform}(40,70)\).

Question. Find: (a) \(P(X>55)\), (b) \(P(45\le X\le 60)\). Interpret both answers.

The total interval length is \[b-a=70-40=30.\]

\[P(X>55)=\frac{70-55}{30}=\frac{15}{30}=\frac{1}{2}=0.5.\]

\[P(45\le X\le 60)=\frac{60-45}{30}=\frac{15}{30}=\frac{1}{2}=0.5.\]

Interpretation. A match has a \(\frac{1}{2}\) chance to last more than 55 minutes, and also a \(\frac{1}{2}\) chance to last from 45 to 60 minutes.

The minimum temperature (in \(^\circ\text{C}\)) is modeled as uniform from \(-8\) to \(4\). Let \(X\sim \text{Uniform}(-8,4)\).

Question. Find: (a) \(P(-5\le X\le 1)\), (b) \(P(X>-2)\) using complement reasoning. Interpret each result.

The total interval length is \[b-a=4-(-8)=12.\]

\[P(-5\le X\le 1)=\frac{1-(-5)}{12}=\frac{6}{12}=\frac{1}{2}=0.5.\]

Use the complement of \(X\le -2\):

\[P(X>-2)=1-P(X\le -2)=1-\frac{-2-(-8)}{12}=1-\frac{6}{12}=\frac{1}{2}.\]

Interpretation. There is a \(\frac{1}{2}\) chance the minimum temperature is from \(-5^\circ\text{C}\) to \(1^\circ\text{C}\), and a \(\frac{1}{2}\) chance it is above \(-2^\circ\text{C}\).

A video call starts at a random minute between 50 and 110 minutes after 3:00 p.m. Let \(X\sim \text{Uniform}(50,110)\).

Question. Find: (a) \(P(70\le X\le 95)\), (b) \(P(X<65\ \text{or}\ X>100)\). Interpret both probabilities.

The total interval length is \[b-a=110-50=60.\]

\[P(70\le X\le 95)=\frac{95-70}{60}=\frac{25}{60}=\frac{5}{12}.\]

For the disjoint union:

\[P(X<65\ \text{or}\ X>100)=\frac{65-50}{60}+\frac{110-100}{60}=\frac{15}{60}+\frac{10}{60}=\frac{25}{60}=\frac{5}{12}.\]

Interpretation. The call starts between minutes 70 and 95 with probability \(\frac{5}{12}\), and has the same probability of starting very early or very late.

A streaming app buffer time is uniformly distributed between 0 and 15 seconds. Let \(X\sim \text{Uniform}(0,15)\).

Question. Find: (a) \(P(X\ge 12)\), (b) \(P(4\le X\le 10)\). Interpret each answer in context.

The total interval length is \[b-a=15-0=15.\]

\[P(X\ge 12)=\frac{15-12}{15}=\frac{3}{15}=\frac{1}{5}=0.2.\]

\[P(4\le X\le 10)=\frac{10-4}{15}=\frac{6}{15}=\frac{2}{5}=0.4.\]

Interpretation. There is a \(\frac{1}{5}\) chance buffering lasts at least 12 seconds, and a \(\frac{2}{5}\) chance it lasts between 4 and 10 seconds.
