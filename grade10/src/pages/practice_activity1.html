A small café is considering whether to introduce a new line of artisan desserts. The owner has two options:

• Launch the dessert line, in which case profits depend on demand:   – High demand → 40 thousand USD   – Low demand → 10 thousand USD
• Keep the current menu, yielding 25 thousand USD regardless of demand.

Market research indicates: • Probability of high demand: 0.60 • Probability of low demand: 0.40

Question: Make a decision on the best option based on three perspectives: safe behavior (Maximin), balanced behavior (Expected Value), and risky behavior (Maximax).

A renewable-energy company is evaluating its energy production strategy for the coming year. Three weather conditions are possible:

• Windy • Sunny • Cloudy

The company is comparing three strategies: 1. Build wind turbines 2. Build solar farms 3. Build a balanced hybrid system

Expected profits (in million USD):
• Wind turbines → 85 (Windy), 40 (Sunny), 25 (Cloudy) • Solar farms → 30 (Windy), 90 (Sunny), 20 (Cloudy) • Hybrid system → 60 (Windy), 60 (Sunny), 55 (Cloudy)

Weather probabilities: • Windy → 0.30 • Sunny → 0.50 • Cloudy → 0.20

Question: Make a decision on the best strategy based on three perspectives: safe behavior (Maximin), balanced behavior (Expected Value), and risky behavior (Maximax).

A multinational logistics firm must redesign its global shipping network for the next five years. The firm is considering three network configurations:

1. Centralized: A single mega-hub in East Asia 2. Regional: Three hubs (Americas, Europe, Asia) 3. Adaptive AI–Driven: Dynamic routing using machine-learning models

Three volatility scenarios are possible:
• Low Volatility — stable trade flows • Medium Volatility — moderate regional disruptions • High Volatility — significant unpredictability

Expected profits (million USD):
• Centralized → 210 (Low), 130 (Medium), –40 (High) • Regional → 180 (Low), 160 (Medium), 70 (High) • AI–Driven → 150 (Low), 175 (Medium), 140 (High)

Scenario probabilities: • Low → 0.25 • Medium → 0.45 • High → 0.30

Question: Make a decision on the best configuration based on three perspectives: safe behavior (Maximin), balanced behavior (Expected Value), and risky behavior (Maximax).

The maximax rule ignores probabilities and selects the alternative with the highest possible payoff. \[ \max_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Launch}, S_i) = 40,\quad \max_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Keep}, S_i) = 25 \] Because 40 is the largest best payoff, the maximax (risk-seeking) recommendation is launch desserts.
↑
The maximin rule focuses on the worst-case payoff of each alternative and picks the largest of those worst cases. \[ \min_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Launch}, S_i) = 10,\quad \min_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Keep}, S_i) = 25 \] Since 25 exceeds 10, the maximin (risk-averse) choice is keep the current menu.
↑
We frame the decision as choosing the menu strategy that maximizes the café's objective. Alternatives are Launch desserts or Keep menu; the uncertain states are high or low demand with probabilities 0.60 and 0.40. The optimal decision under each criterion uses the following calculations. \[ \begin{aligned} EV_{\text{Launch}} &= 0.60(40) + 0.40(10) \\ &= 0.60\cdot40 + 0.40\cdot10 \\ &= 24 + 4 = 28 \\ EV_{\text{Keep}} &= 0.60(25) + 0.40(25) \\ &= 0.60\cdot25 + 0.40\cdot25 \\ &= 15 + 10 = 25 \end{aligned} \] \[ \begin{aligned} \max_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Launch}, S_i) &= \max\{40, 10\} = 40 \\ \max_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Keep}, S_i) &= \max\{25, 25\} = 25 \end{aligned} \] \[ \begin{aligned} \min_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Launch}, S_i) &= \min\{40, 10\} = 10 \\ \min_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Keep}, S_i) &= \min\{25, 25\} = 25 \end{aligned} \] Summary of recommendations across decision criteria
  • Maximax favors launch desserts (highest payoff of 40).
  • Maximin favors keep the current menu (best worst-case payoff of 25).
  • Expected value favors launch desserts (EV of 28).
Comparing the criteria shows that the launch option leads on optimistic and average performance, while keeping the menu provides stronger downside protection.

The firm wants the production mix that best supports its profit goal. Alternatives are wind, solar, or a hybrid system and the uncertain states are windy, sunny, or cloudy with respective probabilities 0.30, 0.50, and 0.20. Optimizing by expected value yields: \[ \begin{aligned} EV_{\text{Wind}} &= 0.30(85) + 0.50(40) + 0.20(25) \\ &= 0.30\cdot85 + 0.50\cdot40 + 0.20\cdot25 \\ &= 25.5 + 20 + 5 = 50.5 \\ EV_{\text{Solar}} &= 0.30(30) + 0.50(90) + 0.20(20) \\ &= 0.30\cdot30 + 0.50\cdot90 + 0.20\cdot20 \\ &= 9 + 45 + 4 = 58 \\ EV_{\text{Hybrid}} &= 0.30(60) + 0.50(60) + 0.20(55) \\ &= 0.30\cdot60 + 0.50\cdot60 + 0.20\cdot55 \\ &= 18 + 30 + 11 = 59 \end{aligned} \] \[ \begin{aligned} \max_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Wind}, S_i) &= \max\{85, 40, 25\} = 85 \\ \max_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Solar}, S_i) &= \max\{30, 90, 20\} = 90 \\ \max_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Hybrid}, S_i) &= \max\{60, 60, 55\} = 60 \end{aligned} \] \[ \begin{aligned} \min_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Wind}, S_i) &= \min\{85, 40, 25\} = 25 \\ \min_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Solar}, S_i) &= \min\{30, 90, 20\} = 20 \\ \min_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Hybrid}, S_i) &= \min\{60, 60, 55\} = 55 \end{aligned} \] Summary of recommendations across decision criteria
  • Maximax favors solar farms (highest payoff of 90).
  • Maximin favors the hybrid system (best worst-case payoff of 55).
  • Expected value favors the hybrid system (EV of 59).
The comparison shows solar excels only in the best-case outcome, while the hybrid strategy dominates on downside protection and average profitability.

The logistics firm frames its decision as selecting the network that maximizes long-run profit. Alternatives are centralized, regional, or AI–driven and the uncertain states are low, medium, or high volatility with probabilities 0.25, 0.45, and 0.30. Using expected value to target optimality: \[ \begin{aligned} EV_{\text{Centralized}} &= 0.25(210) + 0.45(130) + 0.30(-40) \\ &= 0.25\cdot210 + 0.45\cdot130 + 0.30\cdot(-40) \\ &= 52.5 + 58.5 - 12 = 99 \\ EV_{\text{Regional}} &= 0.25(180) + 0.45(160) + 0.30(70) \\ &= 0.25\cdot180 + 0.45\cdot160 + 0.30\cdot70 \\ &= 45 + 72 + 21 = 138 \\ EV_{\text{AI}} &= 0.25(150) + 0.45(175) + 0.30(140) \\ &= 0.25\cdot150 + 0.45\cdot175 + 0.30\cdot140 \\ &= 37.5 + 78.75 + 42 = 158.25 \end{aligned} \] \[ \begin{aligned} \max_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Centralized}, S_i) &= \max\{210, 130, -40\} = 210 \\ \max_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Regional}, S_i) &= \max\{180, 160, 70\} = 180 \\ \max_i \text{Payoff}(\text{AI}, S_i) &= \max\{150, 175, 140\} = 175 \end{aligned} \] \[ \begin{aligned} \min_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Centralized}, S_i) &= \min\{210, 130, -40\} = -40 \\ \min_i \text{Payoff}(\text{Regional}, S_i) &= \min\{180, 160, 70\} = 70 \\ \min_i \text{Payoff}(\text{AI}, S_i) &= \min\{150, 175, 140\} = 140 \end{aligned} \] Summary of recommendations across decision criteria
