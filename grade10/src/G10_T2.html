A decision analysis starts by turning a story into a structured model. You identify the choices, describe the uncertainty, and then apply decision rules to compare alternatives.

  1. Define the decision alternatives: list the strategies you can actually choose.
  2. Describe the states of nature: identify the external situations you cannot control.
  3. Build the payoff table: record the consequence of each alternative in every state.
  4. Choose the decision rule: match the rule to the information available and the risk attitude.
  5. Justify the final recommendation: compare results across criteria and explain the trade-offs.

How the main examples use this process:

  • Examples 1.1 and 1.2 show the full workflow when probabilities are known and when rules without probabilities are also considered for comparison.
  • Examples 2.1 and 2.2 expand the payoff table by combining independent events, such as demand and costs, before applying the same decision rules.
  • Example 2.3 models paired decisions (revenue and cost choices) and uses the same criteria to highlight how structure affects the final recommendation.

Decision criteria reminders:

  • Bayes EMV: uses probabilities to compute expected value and is usually the best guide when probabilities are reliable.
  • Minimax regret: focuses on avoiding the largest missed opportunity when probabilities are missing or uncertain.
  • Maximax: highlights the most optimistic outcome and favors risk-taking strategies.
  • Maximin: protects against the worst case and favors cautious strategies.

Expected Value is an interactive simulation page that reinforces Grade 10 expected value intuition across seven sections.

  • Section 1 — Coin Settings: Configure a biased coin and its probabilities.
  • Section 2 — Dice Settings: Configure a custom die and its probabilities.
  • Section 3 — Simulation Settings: Choose trials and update controls.
  • Section 4 — Coin Outcomes: Display coin outcomes and summary statistics.
  • Section 5 — Dice Outcomes: Display dice outcomes and summary statistics.
  • Section 6 — Coin Convergence Graph: Visualize expected value convergence for the coin.
  • Section 7 — Dice Convergence Graph: Visualize expected value convergence for the die.
